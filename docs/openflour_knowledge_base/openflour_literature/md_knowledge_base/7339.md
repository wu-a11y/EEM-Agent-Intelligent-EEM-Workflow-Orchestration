# 1．Introduction

Reverse osmosis (RO) process which has been broadly applied for water reclamation of effluent from wastewater treatment plants usually generates concentrate accounting for $1 5 { - } 2 0 \%$ of the input stream (Al-Rifai et al., 2Oo7). RO concentrate (ROC) may contain elevated salts and a high level of organic pollutants,some of which are toxic and bio-accumulative (Roddick etal., 2O16). Consequently, it poses a threat to the environment and therefore must be treated to reduce or even eliminate the negative impact associated with the organic pollutants (Zhou et al., 2O11). Coal-to-gas brine (CGB) is a typical RO concentrate generated in the booming coal gasification industry in China.Since it contains a high concentration of toxic and bio-accumulative organic compounds,zero liquid discharge (ZLD) of CGB is rigorously required in China to protect environment.

Currently, ZLD technologies for CGB treatment integrate RO, evaporation, and crystallization processes. These technologies are appealing to not only recovering water and salt resources from CGB but also eliminating its negative effect on the environment.Nevertheless,a high concentration of organic pollutants in ROC incurs severe membrane fouling and evaporator corrosion and thus significantly deteriorates the stability of the processes (Marsal et al.,2Ol6).Moreover,if organic compounds in CGB are not significantly removed, the ZLD process will produce considerable quantities of unusable salt mixtures which are classified as hazardous waste and their disposal cost is as high as 500 dollars per ton.

In the past decades,biological and advanced oxidation process (AOPs) have been increasingly investigated for the treatment of ROC (Weng et al., 202O). However, neither single process turned to be very effective for organic removal from the ROC (Bagastyo et al., 2O11; Liu et al.,2O19). For AOPs, various radicals (e.g.,HO.,Cl·,and $\mathrm { C l } _ { 2 } { \bullet } \mathrm { \dot { \scriptscriptstyle } }$ with strong oxidation ability can be generated in the treatment system to degrade refractory organic contaminants in the ROC (Chen et al., 2020; Yang et al.,2Ol6).Nonetheless,the relatively high concentration of organic compounds inevitably incurs high operation cost (Cai et al., 2020).Moreover,various types of organics in the ROC are radical scavengers,which will affect the treatment efficiency of AOPs (Guo et al.,2Ol7). Compared to AOPs,biological process can achieve relatively low treatment cost.Nevertheless,microorganisms and algae used to degrade organic pollutants need to be screened and domesticated to adapt to the high salt environment in ROC,which is still great challenging at present (Umar et al., 2016; Woo et al., 2019). Therefore, to take the best advantage of AOPs and biological process,they need to treat wastewaterswith suitablewaterqualityparameters.

From this perspective,the combination of AOPs and biological process may offer a cost-effective option for CGB treatment.For example,if part of organic components with litte inorganic salts is initially separated from ROC,they can be introduced into a biological reactor for treatment of activated sludge. Even if their biodegradability is poor, they can be treated by chemical oxidation followed by biological treatment as an effective approach.Residual organic matter in ROC can then be subjected to AOPs treatment. Because of the much-reduced concentration of organics and radical scavengers,the treatment cost of AOPs is anticipated to be significantly decreased.For the separation of CGB,this can be achieved via the adsorption-regeneration process,that is the adsorbent eluate for biological treatment and the adsorption effluent for AOPs treatment (Yang et al., 2013).

To this end,a new multistage system including an adsorption—regeneration process and an $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ process was designed for the removal of organic compounds from coal-to-gas brine (CGB). Macroporous resin,activated carbon,and activated alumina were used to selectively concentrate part of organics that might quench reactive radical species from CGB without changing its conductivity. Considering the high concentration of Cl' in the adsorption efluent,an electrochemicallydriven UV/chlorine $\scriptstyle \left( \mathbf { E } { - } \mathbf { U } \mathbf { V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 } \right)$ process was selected for AOP treatment.The $\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ process was designed to provide enhanced oxidation of the residual organic compounds by utilizing Cl' in CGB to produce a highly active oxidation agent.In addition,an effective regeneration method was proposed for the exhausted resin,and the treatability and biodegradability of the resin eluate was assessed to establish a complete cost-effective process for CGB treatment.

# 2.2.Adsorption-regeneration experiments

Continuous adsorption-regeneration experiments were conducted in the apparatus illustrated in Fig.1. Before experiments,the LS-109D was filled in a column (diameter: $3 6 . 6 \ \mathrm { m m }$ ,length: $1 7 3 . 3 \ : \mathrm { m m }$ )and the raw CGB was introduced from the bottom of the column using a micro peristaltic pumpata flow rate of 1-BV/h.The LS-1o9D column effluent was discharged from the top of the column and stored in a tank.Because the experiments were conducted to test the adsorption effect of adsorbents on removal of organic and inorganic pollutants,the flow rate was set at a low flow rate of 1BV/h (Hassan et al., 20o3; Lilga et al., 2001).

The exhausted adsorbent LS-1o9D was subjected to in-situ regeneration at $3 6 3 \mathrm { K }$ with a constant flow rate of 2.5-BV/h.The LS-109D column eluate was collected in a tank and mixed for biodegradability analysis.In addition,an ultrasonic cleaning instrument $( 6 0 0 ~ \mathrm { W }$ and $4 0 \ \mathrm { k H z }$ ）was used for enhancing the desorption of organic compounds from the exhausted adsorbent LS-1o9D.The main parameters of the continuous adsorption-regeneration experiments were demonstrated in Text S2.

Static adsorption and desorption experiments for five kinds of adsorbents (i.e.,LS-109D,D4020,X-5,GAA,and GAC) were performed according to the procedures presented in Text S3.

# 2.3.Electrochemically driven UV/chlorine process experiments

Treatment of the LS-109D column effluent by $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l _ { 2 } }$ process was conducted in an electrolysis cell $\mathrm { 1 7 0 ~ m L ) }$ ,which consists of a quartz beaker equipped with a $\mathrm { T i } / \mathrm { T i O } _ { 2 } / \mathrm { I r O } _ { 2 }$ anode and a 304 stainless-steel cathode.The effective area of both electrodes was $4 2 \mathrm { c m } ^ { 2 }$ and the distance between them was $5 \mathrm { m m }$ .Two low-pressure $\mathrm { H } g$ lamps $( 2 5 4 \mathrm { n m } ,$ 9 W) were used to provide $2 5 4 \mathrm { n m }$ UV radiation. The outer wall of the $\mathrm { H } g$ lamps is close to the outer wall of the quartz beaker.The treatment was performed at a current density of $1 0 \mathrm { m A } / \mathrm { c m } ^ { 2 }$ .After 120-minute reaction,the samples were withdrawn for analysis.

To investigate the mechanism of $\mathbf { E } { \mathrm { - } } \mathbf { U } \mathbf { V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ ,the treatment of benzoic acid (BA) aqueous solution $( 5 0 ~ \mathrm { m g / L } )$ was carried out using $3 0 \mathrm { m m o l / L }$ NaCl and $\mathrm { N a _ { 2 } S O _ { 4 } }$ as electrolytes for $2 0 \mathrm { { m i n } }$ ,respectively.In these experiments,BA was used as the probe pollutant which reacts with radicals but not free chlorine (FC) (Cho and Hoffmann, 2015).

# 3.2.Adsorption and desorption efficiency of adsorbents

Fig. 4(a) shows the DOC removal rates resulting from the adsorption of the five adsorbents ranged from $5 3 . 1 3 \%$ to $2 4 . 2 8 \%$ after $3 6 \mathrm { h }$ static adsorption reaction. The order is $\begin{array} { r l } { \mathrm { L S } { - } 1 0 9 \mathrm { D } } & { > \mathrm { X } { - } 5 > \mathrm { D } 4 0 2 0 > \mathrm { G A C } } \end{array}$ $>$ GAA.The adsorption of organic compounds on the five adsorbents is not only positively correlated with their high specific surface area (SSA) but also related to their pore size distribution (PSD)and matrix (Table 2, Fig.S3).The amount of adsorption binding sites is proportional to the SSA of adsorbents (Garcia-Jaramillo et al., 2O15; Samrat et al., 2020; Scordino et al., 2003; Yu et al., 202O).Meso- and macropores provided a great deal of significant flow channels for organic molecules (Aschermann et al., 2018; Corwin and Summers, 2011), which are favorable for the diffusion of dissolved organic matter (DOM) onto the surface of adsorbents.In addition, the aromatic moieties of DOM in CGB are expected to induce $\pi { - } \pi$ interaction with the polystyrene matrix of the resins (Li and Sengupta,1998). The three resins possess not only high SSA but also a polystyrene matrix and abundant meso- and macropore regions (Safaei, 2017; Tang et al., 2O21). The GAC has a similar SSA with the D4020 but without polystyrene matrix,while the GAA has the lowest SSA.Considering these factors,the performance of these absorbents for the adsorption of organic compounds in CGB can be well interpreted. Especially, the LS-1o9D is the most effective for the removal of organic compounds from CGB due to its largest SSA,abundant meso-and macropore regions,and polystyrene structure that could induce $\pi { - } \pi$ interaction.The correlation coefficients $\mathrm { R } ^ { 2 }$ of Freundlich and Langmuir models are listed in Table S2,Fig.4(b) and Fig.S4. he Freundlich model fits better than the Langmuir model $( \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 } > 0 . 9 9 )$ . This indicates that the adsorption of DOM in CGB by the five adsorbents is multilayer adsorption and dominated by physical adsorption.The $1 / \mathfrak { n }$ value of LS-109D $( 1 / \mathrm { n } < 0 . 5 )$ ）was the lowest,indicating that it was the most effective adsorbent in adsorbing organic matter from CGB.(Andersson et al., 2011).

After being treated by the adsorption process with five adsorbents, the conductivity of the adsorption equilibrium supernatant is very close to that of the raw CGB (Fig.4(c)).This indicates that all five adsorbents had a weak ability to absorb inorganic substances.This may be due to the fact that there are very few adsorption sites on the surfaces of the five adsorbents where ion exchange (Liu et al., 2Oo2; Rengaraj and Moon, 2002),complexation reactions (Sun et al., 2O21) and electrostatic forces (Kyzas et al., 2O19) can occur. In aqueous environments with high concentrations of inorganic salts,itis dificult to effectively decrease the inorganic salt concentration in the CGB by direct adsorption.

The results from the static desorption experiments show that the desorption rate of DOC from the LS-109D was $7 9 . 5 \%$ at $3 6 3 \mathrm { K } ,$ indicating that the exhausted adsorbent LS-1o9D can be regenerated via hot water elution (Fig.4(d)).An increasing temperature is favorable for the desorption of organic compounds from LS-1o9D.This is due to the enhancement of the mobility and internal energy of DOM molecules and the lowering of the dynamic viscosity coefficient of the elution solvent (Cisse et al., 2Ol2).The π-π stacking energies of be benzene ring is about $- \ : 1 . 8 5 \mathrm { \sim } - 1 0 \mathrm { k c a l / m o l }$ much lower than that of the chemical bond (Khrenova et al., 2019; Mishra and Venkatnarayan, 2018). The π-π stacking is unstable in high temperature environments.Ultrasonic aid can also enhance thermal desorption as the presence of ultrasound cavitation could facilitate DOM movement within the LS-1o9D （Tao et al.,2017; Zhang et al., 2016). In addition, Fig. S5 shows the internal pore structure of LS-1o9D.It is found that the pore structure inside the resin was not destroyed by high temperature and ultrasound.

Generally,most coal chemical enterprises generatea considerable amount of waste heat which can be conveniently used to heat the solution.With the thermal desorption process,the regenerant with low concentration of salts will be produced,which could be returned to the secondary biochemical treatment process with minimal effect on activated sludge.If highly acidic or alkali elution was used for regenerating the exhausted adsorbent LS-1o9D,a higher regeneration efficiency could be achieved.Nevertheless,disposal of the produced regenerant witha high salt concentration is difficult.

Considering these arguments,the LS-1o9D might be used as an ideal adsorbent for separating DOM from raw CGB without being accompanied by high concentration of salts.

# 3.3.Adsorption-regeneration cycles

To evaluate the reliability and feasibility of the LS-1o9D for potential applications,cyclic column adsorption-regeneration runs of the LS-109D were performed and the results were demonstrated in Fig.5(a).At the beginning of the adsorption-regeneration cycles,the DOC concentration of the LS-1o9D column effluent was $1 0 4 . 9 9 \mathrm { m g / L }$ with the influent DOC concentration of $1 6 3 . 1 4 \mathrm { m g / L }$ .When the breakthrough point was set as $1 2 6 ~ \mathrm { m g ~ D O C / L }$ (i.e., $C _ { t } / C _ { 0 }$ increased by O.1),the effective treatment volume was about144 BV for the first run.The exhausted adsorbent LS109D was subsequently regenerated in situ by ultrasound-assisted hot water elution with an elution rate of $2 . 5 { \cdot } \mathrm { B V / h }$ at $3 6 3 \mathrm { K }$ In the subsequent three runs,the effective treatment volume was maintained at approximately 124 BV,suggesting that the LS-109D column exhibited a stable DOM removal performance.

# 3.4.Treatment of the LS-109D column efuent by $E – U V / C l _ { 2 }$

Fig. 6(a) shows the results for the treatment of the LS-1o9D column effluent by various processes.UV photolysis process had a negligible effect on the removal of organic matters in the LS-1o9D column effluent with the DOC removal rate of $7 . 3 2 \%$ EAOP was more efficient than the UV photolysis process,however, the DOC removal rate was still low $( 2 7 . 8 8 \% )$ .The $\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ is very effective for the removal of DOM from the effluent with the DOC removal rate of $7 4 . 6 9 \%$ for 120 mins reaction at the current density of $1 0 \mathrm { m A } / \mathrm { c m } ^ { 2 }$ .After the $\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ treatment, the fluorescence peak intensities ofFA,Trp,and Tyr for the LS-1o9D column effluent were reduced by $9 3 . 2 \%$ $7 2 . 7 \%$ ,and $1 0 0 \%$ ,respectively (Fig. 2 (a)).Prior to the treatment by $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ ,the MW of DOC was primarily distributed in 1-10 and $> 1 0 \mathrm { k D a }$ ,accounting for $3 9 . 2 8 \%$ and $3 8 . 4 5 \%$ respectively (Fig.S2(b)).After treatment, the MWof DOC was primarily distributed in $< 1 \mathrm { k D a }$ ,accounting for $6 8 . 7 \%$ (Fig. S2(c)).

The $\mathrm { T i } / \mathrm { T i O } _ { 2 } / \mathrm { I r O } _ { 2 }$ anode exhibited high chlorine evolution reaction activity in NaCl aqueous solution $\mathrm { ( 3 0 ~ m m o l / L ) }$ (Fig.S7).The high chlorine evolution activity of this anode is due to its $\mathrm { I r O } _ { 2 }$ coating (Text. S6 and Figs.S8\~9),which is consistent with the previous study (Hansen et al.,201O; Yang et al.,2016).Utilizing this anode in EAOP for the treatment of the LS-1o9D column effluent is expected to generate much free chlorine (FC,i.e., $\mathrm { C l } _ { 2 }$ ,HClO,and ClO') since the high concentration of Cl exists in the brine.This insignificantly improves the removal of organic matter in the LS-1o9D column effuent though some previous studies reported that the enhanced electrochemical oxidation of organic compounds in the presence of $\mathrm { C l ^ { - } }$ can be attributed to their reaction with FC (Bonfatti et al., 200O; Malpass et al., 2007). This is mainly due to the low reactivity of electrogenerated FC with organic compounds (Fang et al.,2Ol4).Another possible reason is that Cl·and $\mathrm { C l } _ { 2 } \cdot ^ { - }$ ,which are primarily responsible for organic compound degradation (Cho and Hoffmann, 2014; Cho et al., 2014; Park et al., 2009), cannot be significantly generated in single electrochemical system.

$\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ significantly outperforms the UV photolysis process and EAOP,indicating there might be a synergistic effect between UV photolysis and EAOP.To investigate the synergistic mechanism for DOC removal in $\mathrm { E - U V / C l _ { 2 } } ,$ the treatment of BA aqueous solution was conducted by various processes in this study (Fig.6(b)).The photolytic degradation rate of BA in three kinds of solution (BA, ${ \tt B A + }$ NaCl, and $\mathrm { \bf B A } + \mathrm { N a } _ { 2 } \mathrm { S O } _ { 4 } )$ is similar in the UV photolysis process.Thus,BA could be photolyzed, while $C l ^ { - }$ and ${ \mathsf { S O } } _ { 4 } ^ { 2 - }$ could not be converted to $_ { C l \bullet }$ ， $C l _ { 2 } ^ { * - }$ and $S O _ { 4 } ^ { \bullet - }$ under UV irradiation.This suggests that UV irradiation alone cannot take advantage of the high concentration of $\mathrm { C l ^ { - } }$ in brine to generate enough radical species for efficient oxidation of organic matters (Wang etal.,2O2la). The BA degradation in two kinds of solution $\mathrm { ( B A + }$ NaCl and ${ \bf B A } + { \bf N a } _ { 2 } { \bf S O } _ { 4 } ,$ by EAOP with the $\mathrm { T i } / \mathrm { T i O } _ { 2 } / \mathrm { I r O } _ { 2 }$ anode was relatively slow.This further proves that the ability of FC to oxidize organic matter is limited.Moreover,it can be inferred that the electrochemical generation of $S O _ { 4 } ^ { * - }$ ,HO., $O \bullet ^ { - }$ $C l \bullet$ and $C l _ { 2 } ^ { \cdot - }$ was negligible on the $\mathrm { T i } / \mathrm { T i O _ { 2 } / I r O _ { 2 } }$ anode since they are active for the degradation of BA (Guo et al.,2O17). Therefore,it is challenging to degrade organic compounds by using both active chlorine and free radicals at the same time in the EAOP.The development of durable anodes with high activity for both chlorine evolution reaction and radical generation is thought to be an ongoing challenge (Yang et al., 2Ol6). Albeit a multilayer heterojunction anode of SbSn/CoTi/Ir was reported to be active for enhancing the evolution of reactive chlorine and radical generation, its preparation was somewhat complicated (Yang et al.，2O16),which perhaps limits its practical use. The BA removal rates in $\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ using NaCl as electrolyte was $9 8 . 3 6 \%$ within $2 0 \mathrm { { m i n } }$ .This indicates not only FC but also radicals are significantly produced in the system,which greatly improves the degradation of BA in aqueous solution.This also explains that the $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ is more effective in removing organic matters in the LS-1o9D column effluent than the single process.Therefore, $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ could be an alternative way for significantly producing FC, reactive chlorine species (RCS),and reactive oxygen species (ROS) to enhance the removal of organic compounds from industry hypersaline wastewater.

After the treatment by the combination process of the LS-109D adsorption and $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ ，the DOC concentration decreased from $1 6 3 . 4 1 \mathrm { m g / L }$ to $2 6 . 5 8 \mathrm { m g / L }$ .If the CGB was directly treated by E-UV/ $\mathrm { C l } _ { 2 } ,$ the DOC value was reduced to $8 4 . 6 0 \mathrm { m g / L }$ solely (Fig.6(a)). Obviously,the combined process is more efficient for the removal of DOM from the raw CGB than $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l _ { 2 } }$ .According to the above discussion,the adsorption process with the LS-1o9D adsorbent preferentially removes aromatic moieties which have quenching effect of $C l \bullet$ and $C l _ { 2 } ^ { \cdot - }$ (Guo et al., 20l7) and/or low-MW organic compounds which are inactive with hydroxyl radicals (Chen et al., 2017; Sgroi et al., 2021) in CGB.In addition,humic acid exerts an inhibitory effect on the FC degradation of phenolic compounds which existed in the CGB (Xiang et al.,202O).Therefore, the combined processes of LS-109D adsorption and $\mathrm { { E } { - } \mathrm { { U V } / C l _ { 2 } } }$ applied has obvious advantages over $\mathrm { E } { \mathrm { - } } \mathrm { U } \mathrm { V } / \mathrm { C l } _ { 2 }$ alone for the removal of DOM from CGB.

The fluorescence peak intensity of each fluorescent component in the effluent from the combination process was reduced by $> 9 7 \%$ (Fig. 2). The MW of DOC was mainly distributed in $< 1 \mathrm { k D a }$ ，and its DOC concentration was reduced to $1 8 . 2 6 ~ \mathrm { m g / L }$ (Fig.3). This indicates that the DOM characteristics changed from high fluorescence and MW to low fluorescence and MW after the treatment.As the fluorescent components and high-MW DOM are the main components posing a great negative effect on the stable operation of evaporative crystallizers (Liu et al., 2011;Wang et al., 2021b; Zhang et al.,202O), the coupling of the LS-109D adsorption and $\mathbf { E { \mathrm { - } } U V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ is efficient not only for removing DOM in CGB but also for improving the performance of subsequent membrane and evaporative crystallization processes (Liu et al., 2011).

# 3.5.Biodegradability of Influent and Effluentfor the Combined Treatment Processes

To explore the feasibility of biodegradation of organic matter in CGB, the Zahne-Wellens test was used in this study to evaluate the biodegradability of the influent and effluent for the multistage treatment system.Fig.7 illustrates the $\mathrm { D } _ { \mathrm { f } }$ evolution of various water samples during the Zahne-Wellens test.The characteristics of each water sample, including DOC and COD at day O and day 28,are shown in Table S3.

During the 28-d Zahne-Wellens test, the $\mathrm { D } _ { \mathrm { f } }$ values of the raw CGB $( \mathbf { S } _ { 1 } )$ and LS-109D column effluent $\left( \mathbf { S } _ { 2 } \right)$ were $0 . 1 6 \%$ and $0 . 7 0 \%$ ,respectively, indicating that they were essentially not biodegradable.This is mainly due to the high concentration of inorganic salts contained in CGB which could inactivate microorganisms on activated sludge.The DOC concentration of the two water samples decreased slightly in the initial $^ { 3 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ (data not shown) due to the adsorption of activated sludge.

The conductivity of the LS-1o9D column eluate $\left( S _ { 3 } \right)$ was $0 . 2 3 9 \mathrm { m s } /$ cm(Table 1), indicating that the concentration of salts in the LS-109D column eluate was much lower than that of the raw CGB.During the Zahne-Wellens test, the low concentration of salt in solution will not inhibit the inoculated microorganisms.The $\mathrm { D } _ { \mathrm { t } }$ value of the LS-109D column eluate $\left( S _ { 3 } \right)$ was $3 4 . 5 7 \%$ and the COD concentration decreased from 821.7 to $4 6 7 . 9 5 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { O } _ { 2 } / \mathrm { L }$ in the Zahne-Wellens test,indicating its biodegradability is much higher than that of the raw CGB and LS-109D column effluent.According to the previous study,water samples may be considered not easily biodegradable when the $\mathrm { D } _ { \mathrm { f } }$ value is $< 4 0 \%$ (Herraiz-Carbone et al., 202O). Thus, the organic matter in the LS-109D column eluate still could not be eficiently degraded by the microorganisms in activated sludge though its biodegradability of the LS-109D column eluate was higher than that of $s _ { 1 }$ and $S _ { 2 }$ .This suggests that some of the bio-refractory organic matters were transferred from the raw CGB to the LS-1o9D column eluate by the adsorption-regeneration process.

Fig.7 and Table.S3 show the pretreatment of ozonation significantly enhances the biodegradability of the LS-1o9D column eluate as the Dt of ${ \bf S } _ { 3 } { \sim } { \bf S } _ { 7 }$ was higher than that of $S _ { 3 }$ .After the treatment by ozonation, the fluorescence peak intensities of FA,Trp,and MHA in the LS-1o9D column eluate were all greatly reduced and most of the high-MW organic substances were converted into low-MW organic substances (Fig.2(a) and Fig.3).Most of these organic compounds are bio-refractory (Liu et al.,201l; Zhang et al., 202O). Therefore,ozone can be used as a pretreatment process to improve the biodegradability of the LS-109D column eluate.Especially，the $\mathrm { D } _ { \mathrm { f } }$ value of $s _ { 7 }$ was $8 9 . 4 9 \%$ For Zahne-Wellens test of $\mathbf { S } _ { 7 }$ ,the COD of the LS-109D column eluate could be reduced from 686.0 to $6 5 . 8 9 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { O } _ { 2 } / \mathrm { L }$ ,which was lower than the emission standard of pollution for coking chemical industry (GB 16171-2Ol2) discharge limits for wastewater treatment plants effluents, i.e., COD concentration of $8 0 \mathrm { m g } 0 _ { 2 } / \mathrm { L }$ (Moreira et al., 2015). This indicates Sample $s _ { 7 }$ was withdrawn from the ozonation process after $6 0 \mathrm { { m i n } }$ reaction and could be selected as the optimal endpoint for the ozonation treatment.

# 4.Conclusion

The combination of adsorption-regeneration process, $\mathbf { E } { \mathrm { - } } \mathbf { U } \mathbf { V } / \mathbf { C l } _ { 2 }$ and biological processes can provide the effective treatment of raw CGB.In the adsorption-regeneration process using the LS-1o9D resin as adsorbent,the DOM in CGB was significantly removed with the effective treatment volume of about 122 BV,and the DOC concentration was reduced from $1 6 3 . 4 1 \mathrm { m g / L }$ to $1 0 4 . 9 9 \mathrm { m g / L }$ .After being treated by E$\mathrm { { U V } } / \mathrm { { C l } } _ { 2 }$ ,the DOC concentration of raw CGB was reduced to $2 6 . 5 8 \mathrm { m g / L }$ The effluent may pose less effect on the operation of the subsequent membrane and evaporation crystallizer due to the great reduction of DOM. The resultant column eluate generated from the hot water regeneration process contained a low concentration of salt and showed lowbiodegradability.It can be converted to high biodegradability and can be easily disposed of by chemical process after pretreated by Ozonation.
