The occurrence and distribution of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) in a city of Central China were determined in the settled dust and its extracted water phase from different land use types and bus stops in Nanchang City.The physicochemical properties of its water extracted disolved organic mater (WEOM) were characterized to investigate the effect offluorescence organic mater onthe dust-water partitioning coefficients $( K _ { d } )$ using three-dimensional excitation-emisson matrix fluorescence spectroscopy combined parallel factor analysis.Results showed that the range of $\scriptstyle \sum \mathrm { P A H s }$ in settled dust and the extracted water phase was O.O5-15.92 $\mu \mathbf { g } { \cdot } \mathbf { g } ^ { - 1 }$ and $2 { \ - } 2 1 1 \ \mathrm { n g } { \cdot } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ ,respectively.These PAHs mostly came from the combustion of biomass.The risk assessment showed that PAHs in dust had no obvious health risk (less than the magnitude of $1 0 ^ { - 6 } \textcircled { }$ . Additionally, the high molecular weight (HMW) PAHs and the low molecular weight (LMW)PAHs were preferentially adsorbed by dust and the dissolved portion,respectively.It was confirmed by the relatively high $1 0 g K _ { d }$ values of 4.23 for the HMW-AHs.Pearson correlation analysis suggested that the higher concentration of dissolved organic carbon and humic-like substance were in favor of PAHs in dust released into waters.This study can provide information on polution control when considering the impact of fluorescent organic matter on the fate and transport of PAHs.

# 1．Introduction

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs),a typical semi-volatile and persistent organic pollutants,are generally identified as the priority pollutants due to the high toxicity including teratogenicity, carcinogenesis,and mutagenicity (Durant et al.,1996). Because of the high hydrophobicity and octanol-water partitioning coefficient,PAHs tend to adsorb onto the particles in the environment (Parajulee et al.,2017). Thereinto,the settled dust is one of the important carriers for PAHs and can adsorb and diffuse PAHs into the waters (Qishlaqi and Beiramali, 2019).With the continuous accumulation of PAHs in the dust, they may pose a threat to urban ecosystems and human health when exceeding the environmental standards (Najmeddin et al.,2O18).Hence, it's crucial to investigate the fate of PAHs in dust for environmental protection and human health (Gope et al.,2Ol8).At present, sufficient attention has been paid to PAHs in the street dust (Zhen et al., 202O) and from the different land use types (Wang et al.，2O21),but the scarcity of information was about PAHs in the bus stop.

The bus stop is an important portion of the urban traffic system, where pedestrians and passengers are inevitably exposed to pollutants when passing or waiting at the bus stop.It had been found that the levels of the dust were relatively high with a lot of bus stops (Han and Naeher, 2006).Moreover, the pollution of the bus stop is not only affected by the traffics,but also by the buses which could release multiple contaminants during the getting on and off the bus.A relevant study showed that PAHs in dust from the bus could beas high as nearly $1 0 0 ~ \mu \ g \cdot \ g ^ { - 1 }$ (Gao et al., 2015).And compared with walking $( 2 2 { - } 3 7 5 ~ \mathrm { n g } { \cdot } \mathrm { m } ^ { - 3 } )$ ）and taking the subway $( 1 6 { - } 8 3 \mathrm { n g } { \cdot } \mathrm { m } ^ { - 3 } )$ ,a higher PAH concentration was found for the dust from the buses $( 4 9 - 1 5 6 ~ \mathrm { n g } \cdot \mathrm { m } ^ { - 3 } )$ (Yan etal.,2015). These results suggested that buses are one of the significant sources of PAHs.At present,the studies of PAHs in setled dust at the bus stop were mainly focused on their concentrations and the health risks (Wu et al., 2020), while few attentions were paid to the desorption and distribution of PAHs from the dust to the aquatic environments.

In fact,PAHs adsorbed by dust can be distributed to aquatic environments to varying degrees owing to their physicochemical properties (Kalmykova et al., 2O13). Furthermore,PAH contamination is widespread in urban stormwater runoff,with a high concentration in some cases $\boldsymbol { \Sigma 1 6 }$ PAHs ranged from $1 4 7 ~ { \mu \mathrm { g } } { \cdot } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ to $3 6 3 7 \ \mu \mathrm { g } { \cdot } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 } )$ (Awonaike et al.,2021; Shi et al., 2017).After entering into the waters,PAHs would not only increase their bioavailability,but further pollute the other environments with the stormwater runoff process,thereby posing a potential risk to the aquatic ecology and human water supply (Zeng etal.,2O18). Consequently,it is necessary to investigate the mechanism of the migration and transformation of PAHs between solid and water phases.Nevertheless,current studies on the partition of PAHs between solid and water phases are mostly focused on sediments and soils (Wu et al., 202O; Zhang and Diao, 2018), rather than the dust. Besides, it was shown that the fate and transport of PAHs were affected by the partitioning behavior between water and dissolved organic carbon (DOC) (Kimand Kwon, 2OlO).A similar result found that a significant positive correlation existed between the concentrations of 4-6 rings PAHs and the DOC content (Moeckel et al., 2Ol4).Additionally, fluorescent dissolved organic matter (FDOM),whose spectral characteristics could reveal the composition of DOM(Liu et al., 2018b), was verified to play a vital role in the partition of PAHs between the solid-water phase (Tang et al.,2Ol6).Based on these results,more attention needs to be paid to the physicochemical properties of DOM when investigating the distribution of PAHs between the dust and the waters due to the diversity and complexity of various sources of DOM in the dust.

Nanchang City,one of the important provincial capitals in Central China,has experienced rapid economic and social development in recent years,with a significant increase in motor vehicles and population.Different pollutants emitted from vehicle exhaust are easily adsorbed by dust,which will threaten human health (Yan etal., 2020). Hence,settled dust samples from the different land use typesand the bus stops in Nanchang City were collected in this study.PAHs in dust and the extracted water phase from dust were both detected to investigate the distribution of PAHs in the dust-water system.The objectives of this study were to:1) detect the concentration of PAHs in dust and assess the corresponding risk;2） examine the concentration of PAHs in the extracted water phase and explore the distribution behavior of PAHs between the dust and its extracted water phase;3) characterize the physicochemical properties of the water extracted dissolved organic matter (WEOM) from the dust to investigate the effect of fluorescence organic matter on the dust-water partitioning behavior.

# 2.Materials and methods

# 2.1．Sample collection and treatment

Seventy-seven settled dust samples were collected in Nanchang City from March to May in 2017.The different land use types were selected including university,residential buildings,forest parks,traffic roads, railway stations,industrial zone,and bus stops.The specific distribution and description of the sampling sites are presented in Fig.1 and Table S1. The sunny and windless weather was selected when sampling, with no heavy rainfall for at least one week before sampling.Each settled dust sample consisted of a mixture of five subsamples which were collected evenly from each sampling site with a brush,then mixed and put into the same plastic self-sealing bag.After freeze-drying,the dust samples were comminuted and passed through a 1oo-mesh sieve, then stored ina desiccator.

The extracted water phase was extracted by adding $0 . 5 \ : g$ sieved settled dust and $1 0 ~ \mathrm { m L }$ of ultrapure water into a centrifuge tube, then the mixture was shaken in dark for $2 4 \mathrm { h }$ .After reaching the equilibrium, the samples were centrifuged for $1 0 \mathrm { { m i n } }$ at $3 5 0 0 \mathrm { r } { \cdot } \mathrm { m i n } ^ { - 1 }$ . The supernatant was filtered through $0 . 4 5 \mu \mathrm { m }$ filters (PVDF,Millipore,USA),and the filtrates including WEOM were stored at $4 \%$ in dark for subsequent testing.

# 2.2.PAHs extraction and analysis

The PAHs in dust were extracted using an accelerated solvent extractor (ASE 35o; Dionex) in duplicate according to the previous study (Peng et al., 2012). Briefly, $0 . 5 \ : g$ dust samples with diatomite were filled into a $2 2 \mathrm { m L }$ extraction tank and then extracted with a mixture of dichloromethane and acetone mixture $( 1 { : } 1 \ \mathbf { v } / \mathbf { v } )$ .The cleanup procedure followed the EPA method 3630 C.While PAHs in the extracted water phase was extracted by solid-phase extraction (SPE) with Oasis HLB cartridges $\mathrm { \Delta }$ ，Waters).After preconditioned with $6 \mathrm { m L }$ methanol and $1 0 ~ \mathrm { m L }$ Milli-Q water,the water was introduced into the SPE system. Finally, the cartridges were dried and eluted with dichloromethane.All extractions of both dust and waters were concentrated to $1 . 5 ~ \mathrm { m L }$ and finally exchanged to n-hexane.

# 2.3.Determination of total organic carbon and dissolved organic carbon

Total organic carbon (TOC) content in the dust was determined by $\mathrm { K _ { 2 } C r _ { 2 } O _ { 7 }  – F e S O _ { 4 } }$ method according to China National Standards (GB 9834-88).Dissolved organic carbon (DOC) concentration was determined using Shimadzu TOC-L analyzer (Japan).

# 2.4.Three-dimensional excitation emission matrix (3D-EEM) fluorescence spectroscopy analysis

3D-EEMs of WEOM were characterized by a Hitachi F-710o fluorescence spectrophotometer (Hitachi,Tokyo,Japan） over the wavelength ranges of $2 0 0 { - } 4 5 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ for excitation $\left( \operatorname { E x } \right)$ at $5 \mathrm { n m }$ intervals and $2 5 0 { - } 6 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ for emission $\left( \operatorname { E m } \right)$ at $2 \mathrm { n m }$ increments.The PMT voltage was adjusted to $7 0 0 \mathrm { V } .$ Scan speed was set at $1 2 , 0 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m \cdot m i n } ^ { - 1 }$ $5 \mathrm { n m }$ slit was selected for both Ex and Em.The samples at different concentrations were diluted with Milli-Q water to reach the absorbance below O.3 at $2 5 4 ~ \mathrm { { n m } }$ to avoid the inner filter effect (Ohno, 2002).

# 2.5. Data analysis

# 2.5.1．Parallel factor analysis (PARAFAC)

The fluorescence components in 3D-EEMs of the WEOM were identified by the PARAFAC model,with the DOMFlour toolkit of Matlab 2010a (Stedmon and Bro, 2Oo8). Before modeling, Raman and Rayleigh scattering were removed from the data using the method in the previous study through Matlab 2010a (Zepp et al., 20O4).All results were converted into Raman units (r.u.) according to the method described in the previous study (Lawaetz and Stedmon,2Oo9).After examining the leverage of using DOMFluor,the outliers (samples 1,21,and 41) were identified and deducted from the dataset. The split-half analysis and analysis of residuals and loadings were used to ensure their reliability (Stedmon et al., 2003). $\operatorname { F } _ { \mathrm { m a x } }$ (r.u.),the highest fluorescence intensity of each derived component,is used to represent the concentration of the

PARAFAC component.

Additionally, $\mathrm { F n } ( 2 8 0 )$ indicates the relative concentration of the protein-like substance using the maximum fluorescence intensity of Em $= 3 4 0 – 3 6 0 \mathrm { n m }$ at $\mathbf { E x } = 2 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ .While $\mathrm { F n } ( 3 5 5 )$ describes the relative consistency of humic substances using the maximum fluorescence intensity of $\mathrm { E m } = 4 4 0 { - } 4 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ at $\mathbf { E x } = 3 5 5 \mathrm { n m }$ (Zhang et al., 2009). And $\operatorname { F n } ( 2 8 0 )$ and $\mathrm { F n } ( 3 5 5 )$ are the indicators of the autochthonous and terrestrial DOM, respectively (Zhang et al., 2009).

# 2.5.2.The partitioning coeficient of PAHs between the dust and the extracted water phase

The partitioning coefficient $( K _ { d } )$ representing the distribution behavior of PAHs between dust and the extracted water phase was calculated as follows (Zhang et al., 2015):

$$
K _ { d } = \frac { C _ { s } } { C _ { w } }
$$

where $\mathrm { C } _ { \mathrm { { s } } }$ and $\mathrm { C } _ { \mathrm { w } }$ is the PAH concentrations in dust $( \mathbf { n g } { \cdot } \mathbf { k g } ^ { - 1 } )$ and the extracted water phase $( \mathbf { n g } { \cdot } \mathbf { L } ^ { - 1 } )$ , respectively.

$$

# 3.1.TOC,DOC and fluorescence properties of dust and the extracted water phase

# 3.2.Distribution and risk assessment of PAHs in dust

# 3.2.1. Occurrence and distribution of PAHs in dust

The concentrations of PAHs in settled dust from different land use types in Nanchang City are shown in Table 1,where ten PAHs were detected,including 6 LMWPAHs (Nap,Acy,Ace,Fluo,Phe,and An) and 4HMW PAHs (Fl,Py,BaA,and Chry).The total PAHs content $( { \sum } \mathrm { { P A H s } } )$ varied from 0.05 to $1 5 . 9 2 \mu \mathrm { g } { \cdot } \mathrm { g } ^ { - 1 }$ ，with a mean value of $1 . 1 4 ~ \mu \mathbf { g } { \cdot } \mathbf { g } ^ { - 1 }$ Except for BaA $( 9 0 \% )$ and Chry $( 6 0 \% )$ ,the detection frequencies of the other eight detected PAHs were $1 0 0 \%$ .As illustrated in Table 1,the highest average concentration of individual PAH in settled dust of Nanchang City was Phe $( 1 . 8 4 \mu \mathrm { g } { \cdot } \mathrm { g } ^ { - 1 } )$ ,followed by Py and BaA with the mean concentrations of 1.44 and 1.41 $( \mu \mathbf { g } { \cdot } \mathbf { g } ^ { - 1 } )$ ,respectively. Thus,Phe, Py,and BaA were the major components of PAHs in dust from Nanchang City.The concentrations of PAHs from different land use types were ordered as follows: traffic road $>$ residentialbuilding $\approx$ railway station $>$ industrial zone $>$ university $>$ forest park,which demonstrated that traffic emission was the main source of PAHs in the dust. Moreover, LMW- and HMW- PAHs accounted for $3 7 \%$ and $6 3 \%$ of $\textstyle \sum { \mathrm { P A H s } }$ respectively.This may be because of the higher hydrophobicity of HMWPAHs,which are easily adsorbed by dust particles (Zhang et al.,2015). It was verified by the higher concentration of HMW-PAHs from the different land use types (Fig.S4a),especially for that in the traffic roads where the concentration of the HMW-PAHs was more than doubled that of the LMW-PAHs.Overall, the higher $\textstyle \sum$ PAHs were distributed in the north-south and east-west main traffic road,and decreased from both sides,which may be related to the traffic jam and the construction of the subway (Fig. 3a) (Liu et al., 2O18a).In addition, the ratios of the concentrations between LMW-and HMW-PAHs for the dust were small for the traffic roads in the center city;while the counterparts for the surrounding parts of Nanchang City were high,in particular for the south area (Fig.3b).These results suggested that PAHs from the surrounding parts where the highways are widespread were dominant with LMW, while PAHs from the traffic roads in the center city were characteristic with HMW.

# 3.2.2.PAHs source identification

The concentration of PAHs will change after emission due to the different physicochemical properties,but the ratios of PAHs with similar molecular weights are relatively constant so that the concentration ratios of specific PAH isomers could be used to identify the sources of PAHs in the dust (Lv et al., 2014; Yunker et al., 1996).When $\mathbf { A n } / ( \mathbf { A n } \mathbf { + P h e } )$ $< 0 . 1$ $\mathrm { F l } / ( \mathrm { F l } { + } \mathrm { P y } ) < 0 . 4$ andBaA/ $( \mathrm { B a A + C h r y } ) < 0 . 2$ ,PAHs may source from the petroleum; a ratio of Ant/ $\mathbf { \ddot { \tau } } ( \mathbf { A n t + P h e } ) > 0 . 1$ represents that the origin ofPAHscomefromthecombustionsource;when $0 . 4 < \mathrm { F l } / ( \mathrm { F l } + \mathrm { P y } ) < 0 . 5$ ,it represents that PAHs may release from the fossil fuels (vehicle and crude oil) combustion; the ratio of $\mathrm { F l / ( F l / + P y ) }$ $> 0 . 5$ indicates that the source of PAHs may be the combustion of coal, wood,and others;when $0 . 2 < \mathrm { B a A } / ( \mathrm { B a A } + \mathrm { C h r y } ) < 0 . 3 5$ ,itindicates that PAHs come from a mixed source; the ratio of BaA/ $\mathrm { ( B a A + C h r y ) } > 0 . 3 5$ represents that the origin of PAHs released from the combustion source. In this study, the ratio of $\mathtt { A n / ( A n / P h e ) }$ was between 0.12 and 0.54,with an average of O.44; and the ratio of $\mathrm { F l / ( F l / + P y ) }$ and BaA/(BaA+Chry) ranged from 0.08 to 0.58 and 0.04-0.96,respectively,with a mean value of 0.47 and 0.53. These values revealed that multiple sources including petroleum，fossil fuel combustion,and biomass combustion were responsible for the PAHs in settled dust in Nanchang City.In detail (Fig.S5),PAHs in dust from the university originated from petroleum and biomass combustion. The counterparts from the residential building,forest park,traffic road,and railway station were mainly from coal and biomass combustion.Yet the origin of PAHs in the industrial zone was primarily petroleum input, while the bus stops primarily originated from biomass and fossil fuel combustion. The results were similar to those in the literature,i.e.,PAHs from the industrial zone were mainly from petroleum (Grmasha et al., 2O2O). The spatial distribution of the PAHs source in dust indicated that PAHs in most of the dust samples originated from the combustion of biomass $( 6 6 \% )$ ,followed by petroleum $( 1 8 \% )$ (Fig.S6). Combined, PAHs in settled dust in Nanchang City predominantly originated from the combustion of biomass.

# 3.4.Partition of PAHs between dust and the extracted water phase

It has been shown that the partition of PAHs between the solid particles and the extracted waters is significantly related to the solubility and adsorption of PAHs between the two phases,so the partitioning behaviors of PAHs in solid-water systems was studied using the ratios of the certain PAH pairs in dust and its extracted water phase in this study (Fig. S7) (Chen et al., 202O; Shi et al., 2017).For Acy/Phe and Fl/Py pairs,their ratios in the extracted waters (1.30 and 1.92) were much higher than those in the dust (O.22 and O.95).These results indicated that Acy and Fl were more easily released into the soluble phase than Phe and Py,resulting in a higher threat to the aqueous environment. Although the wider range of the ratio of BaA/Chry was observed in dust than that in the extracted waters,the average ratio of this PAH pair was the same in the extracted waters and dust (0.97),which suggested that BaA and Chry had similar distribution behavior in the solid-water system.For Phe/An pairs,their ratios in the extracted waters and dust were 0.46 and 1.6l,respectively,i.e.,the ratios in the extracted waters were much smaller than those in the dust,indicating that An had the better solubility than that of Phe.The results were in agreement with the previous study (Shi et al.，2O17).Overall,Acy and Fl preferred to transfer into the aqueous environment.

Furthermore, the $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ value can be used to measure the distribution pattern of PAHs in dust and the extracted water phase.According to Table 2,the lower and higher $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values were distributed in the university and the traffic roads and industrial zone (3.38,4.O6,and 4.05, respectively).Except for the non-detected compounds,the $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values for all PAHs,HMW-PAHs,and LMW-PAHs were in the range of 1.29-5.61 (4.07,average),1.29-5.61 (4.23,average),and 3.24-5.28 (3.97,average),respectively.That is,the $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values for HMW-PAHs were relatively high,with the highest value for Py.These results indicated that HMW-PAHs were predominant in dust in Nanchang City.In addition,the octanol-water partitioning coefficient $( K o w )$ of PAHs increased as the number of benzene rings increase $\mathbf { ( R = 0 . 9 3 }$ $\mathbf { P } < 0 . 0 1 $ This resulted in a high sorption capacity of HMW-PAHs by the particulate matters and then a lower concentration of HMW-PAHs in waters. Previous studies demonstrated that LMW-PAHs had better solubility than that of HMW-PAHs,making LMW-PAHs preferentially dissolved into the runoff or the other water bodies during rainfall (Zheng et al., 2012). It was consistent with the lower $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values for LMW-PAHs in this study,and this result could help to understand the partitioning behavior and fate of PAHs in dust and the rainfall-runoff Moreover, Fig.3e shows that the lower and the higher $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values were related to the samples from the west part of the Gan River and in the traffic roads of the city center suggesting that PAHs tended to adsorb onto the dust from the traffic roads.Moreover,the lower and the higher ratios of $\mathrm { l o g } K _ { d }$ values between LMW-and HMW-PAHs were found for the center parts and the surrounding parts of Nanchang City (Fig.3f),which was similar to the distribution pattern of the ratios of the concentration between LMW- and HMW-PAHs in dust and the extracted water phase.

# 3.5. Influencing factors

Pearson correlation analysis was performed between the physicochemical properties of WEOM and the $K _ { d }$ values in order to investigate the influence of WEOMon the partitioning behavior of PAHs in the dustwater system (Fig.5). The results showed that all the physicochemical properties of WEOM in Nanchang City were significantly positively correlated with each other,except for C1,which was negatively correlated with $\operatorname { F n } ( 2 8 0 )$ and $\mathrm { F n } ( 3 5 5 )$ .Thereinto,the highest correlation was found between C2 and C3,as well as C2 and Fn (355) $\scriptstyle ( \mathrm { R = } 0 . 7 6 / 0 . 6 7 ;$ $\mathbf { P } < 0 . 0 1 $ )．Moreover，a significant positive intercorrelationwas observed among the $K _ { d }$ values especially for the $K _ { d }$ values of the LMWPAHs.Besides,a significant negative correlation occurred between the physicochemical properties of WEOM (including DOC,C2,C3,and Fn (355))and the $K _ { d }$ values.Nonetheless,no significant relationship existed between the protein-like substance (C1 and Fn (280)) and the $K _ { d }$ values. These results suggested that: 1) the LMW-PAHs readily bound to DOM, thus increased their biological availability in the aqueous environment (Oh etal.,20l6);2) the higher the DOC concentration of the extracted water phase,the smaller the $K _ { d }$ value,i.e.PAHs were more inclined to dissolve in the water when the DOC concentration of the extracted waters was high,which was consistent with the literature (Kim and Kwon,201O)；3) the humic-like components (C2,C3 and Fn (355)) played the preponderant role in the distribution of PAHs between the two phases compared to the protein-like components (Tang et al., 2016), i.e.the higher the intensity of humic-like substance,PAHs were more likely to release to waters.These results would provide more information when exploring the partitioning behavior of PAHs in the dust-water system.

# 4. Conclusions

Characterization of PAHs between settled dust and the extracted water phase in Nanchang City was determined to explore the effect of the physicochemical properties of WEOM on the partitioning behavior of PAHs in the dust-water system.Results revealed that the higher concentrations of $\scriptstyle \sum \mathrm { P A H s }$ in both dust and the extracted water phase were detected in the samples from the traffic roads.These PAHs mostly came from the traffic emissions and combustion of biomass.The risk assessment showed that PAHs in dust had no obvious health risk.Besides,HMW-PAHs were the dominant pollutants in the dust,whereas LMW-PAHs preferred to dissolve in waters due to their higher solubility. Moreover,HMW-PAHs were mainly distributed in the center of the city. It was also found that the humic-like components with higher DOC concentration played a dominant role in the distribution of PAHs between dust and the extracted water phase compared to the protein-like components.These findings in this study could have the potential for wider applications in pollution control when considering the impact of fluorescent organic matter on the fate and transport of PAHs.
