# Factors controlling the distributions of dissolved organic matter in the E ast China Sea during summer

Jeonghyun $\mathsf { K i m ^ { 1 } }$ , Tae‑Hoon $\mathsf { K i m } ^ { 2 \boxtimes }$ , Sang Rul P ark3 , Hyuk Je Lee4 & Jang Kyun Kim5

To determine the distribution of dissolved organic matter (DOM) in the East China Sea (EC S) during the summer, we measured the dissolved organic carbon (DOC) and nitrogen (DON), fuorescent dissolved organic matter (FDOM), and chlorophyll $\pmb { a }$ (Chl. a) in the upper $\pmb { 1 0 0 - m }$ layer of this region during July and September 2015. The DOC $( { \bf r } ^ { 2 } = 0 . 7 2$ and 0.78 in July and September, respectively) and DON $( r ^ { 2 } = 0 . 4 3$ and 0.33) were signifcantly correlated with salinity, suggesting that the river is the primary origin of DOM. However, we found that at a DOC “pulse” under a salinity ranging from 24 to 35, the extrapolating DOC values $( 3 0 4 \pm 1 1 \mu \mathsf { M } )$ were twice higher than those with a salinity of close to 0, as found in a previous study. The excess DOC concentration seemed to be attributed to the microbial metabolism during transport from the estuary based on the good relationships between DOC and marine humic-like FDOM $( \ r ^ { 2 } = 0 . 4 2$ and 0.47), as well as the fuorescence, humifcation, and biological indexes, but showed no correlation with Chl. a. Thus, the results of our study indicate that microbial activities can be a signifcant factor controlling the distribution of DOM in the ECS during summer.

Dissolved organic matter (DOM) in the oceans is one of the largest reservoirs of carbon and nitrogen on Earth, and its distribution and behaviour play crucial roles not only in biogeochemical processes in the ocean but also in the carbon and nitrogen cycles1 . Dissolved organic carbon (DOC), the carbon component of DOM, can be preserved for thousands of years in the deep open oceans with relatively lower and uniform concentrations because more than $9 0 \%$ of DOC is recalcitrant to microbial utilization in the water column2,3 . Tus, this carbon fxed in dissolved organic form could contribute to the sinking of atmospheric $\mathrm { C O } _ { 2 }$ in the ocean through the biological pump4,5 . By contrast, high and signifcant variations in DOC concentrations have been found in the coastal and marginal oceans owing to intense biological activities and large terrestrial inputs6 . Dissolved organic nitrogen (DON), the nitrogen component of DOM, including urea, amino acids, nucleic acids, and amino sugars, is a primary reservoir of reactive nitrogen in the oceans. When dissolved inorganic nitrogen (DIN) is depleted, DON is the dominant pool of fxed nitrogen, and can become a direct energy source for marine microorganisms7 . By the microbial carbon pump, microbial transformation of DOM from labile to recalcitrant state, nitrogen was preferentially removed relative to carbon within the DOM pool, then the recalcitrant DOM shows ultimately the high DOC to DON ratio8 .

Te northwestern Pacifc marginal seas, including the East China Sea (ECS) and the southern sea of Korea, are among the largest continental shelves in the world9 . Tese seas receive huge amounts of freshwater $( 0 . 9 \times 1 0 ^ { 1 2 } \mathrm { { m } ^ { 3 } \mathrm { { y e a r } ^ { - } } }$ 1) from the Changjiang River10 and seawater from the Kuroshio branch water, a strong western boundary current11. Here, the mixing of freshwater driven by the Changjiang River with seawater forms a less saline water mass with a salinity of less than 32, which is known as the Changjiang Diluted Water (CDW). Te CDW front generally extends northeastward toward the vicinity of Jeju Island, Korea during the summer12. Te formation of CDW is not simply the result of a water mixing process, but is also infuenced by the addition of large amounts of terrestrial materials, triggering a subsequent biogeochemical impact on the adjacent seas. Te DIN and DOC fuxes from the Changjiang River to the ECS have been estimated to be $1 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ { \mathrm { i } 2 } \mathrm { g } \mathrm { N }$ year-1 and $4 . 8 \times 1 0 ^ { 1 2 } \mathrm { g C y e a r ^ { - 1 } }$ , respectively13,14. More than $9 9 \%$ of DIN input from the Changjiang River was removed within $2 0 0 \mathrm { k m }$ , perhaps due to rapid consumption of DIN by phytoplankton15. Especially, in this region, the very enhanced chlorophyll a concentration (up to $2 0 \mu \mathrm { g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 } .$ ) was observed over a distance of $1 0 0 \mathrm { k m }$ from the Changjiang River mouth16. Whereas, DON was conservatively mixed $( 3 . 4 \mathrm { - } 1 0 . 1 \mu \mathrm { M }$ [avg. $7 . 0 { \pm } 1 . 3 \ \mu \mathrm { M } ]$ ) during long-range transport $( 2 0 0 - 8 0 0 \mathrm { k m } )$ ) in the surface layer of the ECS during summer15.

A few studies have recently used absorption and fuorescence spectroscopy to characterise DOM in the ECS close to the Changjiang River estuary17–19. However, the behaviours of DOC and DON coupled with optical spectroscopy in the ECS and the southern sea of Korea remain unclear owing to a highly dynamic and complex current system20,21. Excitation-emission matrix fuorescence coupled with the parallel factor analysis (EEM-PARAFAC) has been applied to evaluate the dynamics of fuorescent groups of DOM (FDOM) in coastal environments22–24. Terefore, the objectives of this study were to identify the optical properties of DOM as a tracer of the DOM source and to evaluate the factors controlling the distributions of DOM in the northern ECS and the southern sea of Korea during the summer.

# Discussion

Te relationships of DOC and DON against salinity during the two cruises are shown in Fig. 1. DOC was signifcantly correlated with salinity in July and September 2015 $\mathbf { r } ^ { 2 } { = } 0 . 7 2$ , $\textstyle P ^ { < } { 0 . 0 0 1 }$ and $\mathbf { r } ^ { 2 } { = } 0 . 7 \bar { 8 }$ , $\begin{array} { r } { p < 0 . 0 0 1 , } \end{array}$ ). In addition, DON was weakly correlated with salinity in July and September 2015 $\mathrm { T } ^ { 2 } = 0 . 4 3$ , $\textstyle P ^ { < } { 0 . 0 0 1 }$ and $\mathrm { r } ^ { 2 } { = } 0 . 3 2$ , $\scriptstyle P < 0 . 0 0 1 { \mathrm { , } }$ ) compared to DOC. Tese relationships suggest that the river is the primary origin of DOC and DON. Because the study region is close to Jeju Island and the southern coast of Korea peninsula (Fig. S3), the potential infuence of the land-origin DOM from those places could be expected. However, the DOC concentration in the coastal groundwater of Jeju Island, in which the major source of terrestrial materials is submarine groundwater discharge, was reported to be very depleted $( 2 6 \pm 1 1 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } ^ { 3 8 }$ and $2 1 { - } 5 6 \mu \mathrm { M } ^ { 3 9 } )$ . Assuming the input of DOC from the southern coast of Korea was signifcant, the relationship between DOM and salinity should show a large deviation or two mixing lines due to the efect by the two diferent endmember. Here, we generally observed one mixing line in this study (Fig. 1). In addition, the Changjiang River, which is one of the world’s largest rivers, accounts for approximately $9 0 \%$ of the freshwater input to the adjacent seas and near Korea peninsula40,41. Tese indicate that the Changjiang River is the primary source of DOM, corresponding the previous study15.

However, we found that a DOC “pulse” occurred at a salinity ranging from 24 to 35 (Fig. 1), implying signifcant discrepancies in the DOC concentration between the extrapolated DOC values of the DOC-salinity relationship and the values with a salinity of close to 0, as described in a previous study29. Te extrapolated DOC concentration at zero salinity in this study $( 3 0 4 \pm 1 1 \mu \mathrm { M }$ for both periods) was approximately twice that previously reported in the Changjiang River $( 1 5 6 \pm 2 8 \mu \mathrm { M } ) ^ { 2 9 }$ . Tis indicates that the DOC originating from the Changjiang River was not conservatively mixed in this region. In a previous study, the DOC pulse in the Changjiang estuary and the adjacent ECS shelf occurred within salinity ranges of approximately 19–27 in July $2 0 1 5 ^ { 2 9 }$ . Tey suggested that the increase in DOC was related to the high percentage of particulate organic carbon $( \% \mathrm { P O C } )$ in suspended particulate matter (SPM) by algal accumulation or bloom based on the SPM size spectra measured using laser in-situ scattering and transmissometry.

By contrast, the extrapolated DON concentration at zero salinity $( 2 6 \pm 3 \mu \mathrm { M } )$ was similar to the concentration of DON in the Changjiang River waters (approximately $2 0 \mu \mathrm { M } ) ^ { 4 2 }$ . Tis suggests that the riverine DON was consistent and mixed conservatively in the ECS. Hopkinson, et al.43 showed that the average half-lives of labile and semi-labile DON are 12 and 113 days, respectively, based on incubation experiments using the continental shelf water of the middle Atlantic bright. Here, the CDW from the estuary mouth took $3 3 \pm 2$ days to arrive within the vicinity of Jeju Island $( \sim 4 5 0 \mathrm { k m } )$ ) using a radioactive decay equation of $^ { 2 2 3 } \mathrm { R a }$ activities44. Te conservative behaviour of the Changjiang River-driven DON in this study corresponds to the results from Kwon, et al.15 which showed a conservative mixing pattern of DON of up to $8 0 0 \mathrm { k m }$ from the Changjiang River mouth.

To evaluate the source of the DOC pulse in this study, we compared DOC concentrations with the fuorescent properties of DOM (Fig. 2). Te relationships between DOC and C2 in the ECS showed signifcant positive trends in July and September 2015 $\mathrm { \bf ~ r } ^ { 2 } = 0 . 4 2$ , $\textstyle P ^ { < 0 . 0 0 1 }$ and $\mathbf { r } ^ { 2 } = 0 . 4 7$ , $\scriptstyle P < 0 . 0 0 1 { \mathrm { . } }$ ), whereas the relationships of DOC with C1 and C3 were weak and widely scattered (Fig. 2). Tis demonstrates that marine humic-like FDOM attributed to microbial processing of organic matter can be an important component of the DOC. To support the microbial activity as the origin of the DOC pulse, we calculated the fuorescence index (FI), humifcation index (HIX), and biological index (BIX) from the EEM spectral data. Te FI has been used to identify the source of humic DOM originating from terrestrial $( \sim 1 . 4 )$ or microbial activity $( \sim 1 . 9 )$ 45. In this study, the value of FI ranged from 0.9 to 3.6 (avg. $1 . 9 \pm 0 . 5$ ) in July 2015 and from 1.3 to 3.1 (avg. $1 . 9 \pm 0 . 3 $ ) in September 2015 (Fig. 3), generally indicating the microbial origin of DOC in the water column. A few lower FI values may be associated with the Changjiang River origins. Te values of HIX, an indicator of the degree of DOM humifcation 46, ranged from 0.1 to 0.5 (avg. $0 . 4 \pm 0 . { \overset { . } { 1 } } { }$ ) in July 2015 and from 0.2 to 0.6 (avg. $0 . 4 \pm 0 . { \overset { . } { 1 } }$ ) in September 2015 (Fig. 3). Despite large variations, lower values of less than 4 indicated that DOM in the ECS was related to autochthonous DOM rather than the terrestrial origin47. Te values of BIX, an indicator of autochthonous biological production48, ranged from 1.0 to 2.9 (avg. $1 . 7 \pm 0 . 5$ ) in July 2015 and from 1.0 to 2.7 (avg. $1 . 4 \pm 0 . 3$ ) in September 2015 (Fig. 3). Te average BIX values were higher than 1.0, which correspond to the freshly produced $\mathrm { D O M ^ { 4 8 } }$ , and are similar to the values observed in marine surface oligotrophic waters49.

Although the photodegradation could modify the FDOM indexes during the long-range transport from the Changjiang River, such as signifcant decreasing the degree of DOM humifcation and increasing the BIX value of $\mathrm { D O M } ^ { 5 0 - 5 3 }$ , the values of the indexes between the surface $( 0 ~ \mathrm { m } )$ and subsurface water $( > 0 \mathrm { m } )$ depths in these well-stratifed water columns showed no statistically diferences (Student’s t-test; p-value $= 0 . 0 6$ for HIX, 0.47 for BIX, and 0.35 for FI). In the previous studies, the relatively lower HIX values $( < 2 )$ and higher BIX values $( > 0 . 8 )$ already reported near the Changjiang River mouth54,55, indicating that the in-situ biological production seems to be a signifcant origin of DOM in the ECS. Terefore, the FDOM indexes can be proper indicators to suggest the origin of DOM in the Changjiang River water-seawater mixing zone.

In general, the production of autochthonous DOC from phytoplankton biomass in the ocean is considered to be an important source. Te highest value of primary production $( > 2 , 0 0 0 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { C } \mathrm { m } ^ { - 2 } \mathrm { d } \mathrm { a y } ^ { - 1 } )$ ) has been observed in the inner shelf of the ECS during summer, displaying three-times higher values than during other seasons56. However, in this study, the depth-averaged concentration of Chl. $^ { a }$ , an indicator of phytoplankton biomass57, within the upper $1 0 0 – \mathrm { m }$ depth was relatively lower than those $( 2 { - } 5 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { m } ^ { - 3 } )$ reported for the inner shelf of the ECS during summer58. In this study, weak relationships were shown between Chl. $^ a$ and DOC (Fig. 4). However, it was reported that $\% \mathrm { P O C }$ was positively correlated with Chl. $^ a$ in the Changjiang estuary and adjacent ECS shelf in July $2 0 1 5 ^ { 2 9 }$ . In addition, the bacterial biomass (BB) and production (BP) were high in shelf waters of the ECS (no deeper than $5 0 \mathrm { m }$ ) during summer, and the BB increased with the concentrations of Chl. a within the range of $0 . 0 2 { - } 3 . 0 0 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { m } ^ { - 3 }$ , and temperature within the range of $8 { - } 2 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ , derived from generalised additive models59. Tus, our results indicate that the DOC pulse observed seems to be associated with microbial activities in the seawater rather than the terrestrial and/or phytoplankton-derived DOC.

Te behaviours of DOM in this region was elucidated using a schematic diagram of DOC and the optical properties of DOM (Fig. 5). Te signifcant correlations of DOC and DON against salinity and large variations of the FDOM indexes indicated that the DOM in this region seems to originate from the river and its contribution (i.e., allochthonous source) to the DOM budget is likely to be conservatively diminished during the long-range transport from the river mouth. In addition, the inorganic nutrients and organic matter originating from the Changjiang River may fuel the phytoplankton blooming, followed by POM is produced and released by the phytoplankton and zooplankton. Te POM would be transformed into DOM by the heterotrophic microbial activity (cell lysis and extracellular release), and successively into inorganic nutrients. Based on the optical properties of DOM, the in-situ production of DOM by the bacteria and virus (i.e., autochthonous source) results in the DOC pulse and the enrichment of the marine humic-like FDOM in the CDW region. According to the result of a previous study using the incubation experiment, phytoplankton-derived organic matter is associated with the formation of the humic-like $\mathrm { F D O M } ^ { 6 0 }$ . Tis result supports our arguments. Here, the marine humic-like FDOM and additionally produced DOC in the CDW seems to be simultaneously transported through the ECS. Tus, during the transport of CDW, the contribution of the allochthonous DOM should be decreased, while the autochthonous DOM is likely responsible for the larger fraction of the DOC budget far from the coastal region.

# Conclusions

Te distributions of DOC and DON, together with FDOM and Chl. a, were measured in the upper $1 0 0 – \mathrm { m }$ layer of the ECS during summer. In this study, the DOC and DON concentrations were found to be relatively lower than those in the Changjiang estuary. Te signifcant negative correlation between DOM and salinity suggests that the river is the primary source of DOM in the ECS. Te expected DOC values extrapolated from the DOCsalinity relationship are twice higher than the DOC measurements in the Changjiang River freshwater. However, the extrapolated DON concentrations were similar to the DON end-member with a salinity of close to 0. Te excess DOC relative to the theoretical mixing line implies that the Changjiang River seems to not be the major source of DOC over the ECS during the summer. According to the positive relationship between DOC and the microbial-origin FDOM component, the values of FI, HIX, and BIX, and the relationship between DOC and Chl. a, i.e., the main source of the DOC pulse in the ECS, seem to be associated with microbial DOM. Extensive studies on biogeochemical parameters, such as amino acids, $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C } ,$ and $\delta ^ { 1 4 } \mathrm { C } _ { : }$ , will be necessary to understand the biogeochemical behaviour of DOM in highly dynamic and active oceans.

# Materials and methods

Sampling and hydrological measurements. Te hydrological and biogeochemical surveys were conducted during two diferent periods: July 18 to 20, 2015 aboard the R/V A-Ra of Jeju National University (JNU), Korea, and September 21 to 23, 2015 aboard the R/V Je-Ra of JNU (Fig. S3). Seawater samples for the vertical profles were collected from three to six diferent depths using 12-L Niskin bottles attached to a CTD rosette system. We took the water samples at three diferent water depths $( 0 , 3 0 , 5 0 \mathrm { m } )$ during July 2015 and at six depths (0, 20, 40, 60, 80, 100 m) during September 2015, respectively. Te temperature, salinity, and Chl. a were measured using an SBE911-plus CTD profler with an ECO-FLD fuorometer (Seabird Electronics, USA). To correct the CTD fuorescence as the Chl. a signal, $5 0 0 \mathrm { m l }$ water samples were vacuum fltered through Whatman GF/F flters (pore size of $0 . 7 \mu \mathrm { m } )$ , and then the flters were fash-frozen in liquid nitrogen. Te flters were placed in $1 0 0 \%$ acetone for $^ { 4 \mathrm { h } }$ to extract the pigment. Te Chl. a concentration in extracts was determined using a fuorometer (10-AU-005-CE, Turner Designs, CA) just afer each sampling cruise.

Analysis of DOC and DON . Samples for DOC and DON were vacuum fltered using pre-combusted $5 0 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for $^ { 5 \mathrm { h } }$ ) Whatman GF/F flters. To prevent microbial degradation, the fltrate was acidifed to $\mathrm { p H } 2$ using 6  M HCl, followed by hermetic sealing in pre-combusted ampoules (Wheaton Scientifc, Millville, NJ). Te concentration of DOC was measured by high-temperature catalytic oxidation (HTCO) using a TOC analyser $( \mathrm { T O C - V _ { C P H } }$ , Shimadzu, Japan). Before starting the analysis, the system blank was reduced until the signal from the carbon-free distilled water was stable below the detection limit $( < 5 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } )$ . Te accuracy of the DOC concentration was checked for each sample batch using deep-sea reference (DSR; $4 1 { - } 4 4 \mu \mathrm { M }$ , University of Miami) samples. Te results of our DSR measurement were in good agreement with the consensus values (within $2 \%$ ). Te DON concentration was calculated by subtracting the DIN from the total dissolved nitrogen (TDN), which was measured simultaneously with DOC using the same TOC analyser equipped with a total nitrogen (TN) unit. Te concentration of DON was also verifed with a precision of $2 \% - 3 \%$ of the DSR values $( 3 1 . 0 0 { - } 3 3 . 0 0 \mu \mathrm { M } )$ .

Analysis of FDOM. Samples for FDOM were fltered simultaneously using GF/F flters along with DOC and DON samples. Te fltrate was stored in pre-combusted dark EPA glass vials (Fisher Scientifc, PA) to avoid photo-degradation and kept in a refrigerator at below $4 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ until further analysis. Tree-dimensional fuorescence spectroscopy was applied using a spectrofuorometer (FS-2, SCINCO, Korea) within 3 days of fltration. Te excitation–emission matrix (EEM) fuorescence was measured using scanning emission fuorescence over a wavelength of $2 5 0 { - } 6 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ at $2 \mathrm { - n m }$ intervals with an excitation wavelength of $2 5 0 { - } 5 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ at $5 { \mathrm { - } } \mathrm { n m }$ intervals. Te fuorescence data were normalised every day to those of a quinine sulphate dihydrate standard (unit: QSU). Rayleigh and Raman scattering peaks were removed and replaced with a three-dimensional Delaunay interpolation of the remaining data61. Parallel factor analysis (PARAFAC) modelling for the compilation of 99 EEM data from the two sampling cruises was conducted using the MATLAB R2019b program with the DOMFluor toolbox62. Te precision of the FDOM measurement was $\pm 0 . 0 1$ QSU, and the detection limit was $0 . 1 4 \mathrm { ~ Q S U } ^ { 6 3 }$ . Fluorescence intensities are reported in quinine sulfate units (QSU). Te inner flter efect was not corrected for because the infuence of this artifact was negligible using this spectrofuorometer11,38.

Te FI was calculated as the ratio of the emission fuorescence intensities measured at wavelengths 470 and $5 2 0 \mathrm { n m }$ with an excitation wavelength of $3 7 0 \mathrm { n m } ^ { 6 4 }$ . Te HIX was calculated as the ratio between the sum of the emission fuorescence spectra over the range of $4 3 5 { - } 4 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ and over the range of $3 0 0 { - } 3 4 5 \mathrm { n m }$ and $4 3 5 { - } 4 8 0 \mathrm { n m }$ at an excitation wavelength of $2 5 5 \mathrm { n m } ^ { 6 5 }$ . Te BIX was calculated as the ratio between the fuorescence intensity at the emission wavelength of $3 8 0 \mathrm { n m }$ to that at $4 3 0 \mathrm { n m }$ , at an excitation wavelength of $3 1 0 \mathrm { n m } ^ { 4 8 }$ .

Analysis of DIN . Samples for DIN were fltered simultaneously with the DOM samples. Te fltrate was transferred to acid-clean polypropylene Nalgene bottles and stored in a freezer $( - 2 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } )$ before analysis. Nitrate and nitrite concentrations were photometrically measured from duplicate samples using a nutrient auto-analyser (QuAAtro39, SEAL Analytical, UK). Te accuracies of the nitrate and nitrite concentrations were validated for each sample batch using two diferent certifed reference materials (CRMs): RMNS $( 3 1 . 0 0 \pm 0 . 2 4 \mu \mathrm { M }$ ; KANSO, Japan) and MOOS-3 $( 2 6 . 6 \pm 0 . 3 ~ \mu \mathrm { M }$ ; National Research Council of Canada). Te results of the CRM measurement were in good agreement with the certifed values (within $5 \%$ ). Te detection limits of the DIN components were $0 . 0 1 \mu \mathrm { M }$ .

Data treatment and statistical analysis. To explore the relationships between the variables, Pearson correlation analysis was conducted using SigmaPlot Version 12.0 (Systat Sofware, Inc., CA) (www.systatsof ware.com). Te reported values of the measurement were shown as the average and standard deviation values using Microsof Excel 2016 (Microsof, WA).
