Hydrochar-derived disolved organic matter (HDOM) enters aquatic ecosystems through soil leaching and surface runoff following the application of hydrochar.However,the photochemical behavior of HDOM remains unclear.The photo-transformation of HDOM was analyzed by Fourier transform ion cyclotron resonance mass spectrometry (FT-ICR MS),multiple spectroscopy methods, high-performance liquid chromatography，and combining synchronous fluorescence and Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy with two-dimensional correlation spectroscopy.The results showed that with the increaseofcarbonization temperature,amide I in proteinlike substances were observed to be preferentially photolyzed,and the protein-like substances were more sensitive to low irradiation time,while the duration time of the photochemical behavior of amide I and aliphatic

C-H were more persistent.FT-ICR MS results showed that Nand S-containing molecules,including lignins and lipids were more sensitive to ultraviolet irradiation.Furthermore,the photo-transformation of HDOMs was accompanied by the generation of triple excited state disolved organic matter and singlet oxygen.Our findings will be beneficial for understanding the mechanisms of photo-transformation of HDOM and for predicting the possible behaviors of hydrochar produced at diferent temperatures before large-scale application.

# 1．Introduction

Hydrochar is a carbon-rich product of the hydrothermal carbonization of wet biomass raw materials at low temperatures and high pressure [27].Due to its abundant surface functional groups, porous structure, stable carbon content,and rich organic matter,hydrochar has great potential for hazardous species removal, soil improvement,and carbon sequestration [26,34].Moreover,owing to its low preparation temperature and incomplete carbonization,a large concentrationof hydrochar-dissolved organic matter (HDOM) may be released into aquatic ecosystems through soil leaching and surface runoff upon land utilization [17,18,48]. Owing to its complex composition and structure, HDOM can play different roles in several environmental processes, includingadsorption/dissolution，photochemical/microbial transformation,and polymerization/dispersion [16,46],which may further influence the fates,bio-availabilities,and toxicities of exogenous hazardous species (heavy metals and organic pollutants) in aquatic ecosystems [69].Therefore,research on the environmental behavior of HDOM is necessary before the large-scale environmental application of hydrochar.

Among the aforementioned environmental processes,the phototransformation of DOM significantly affects its properties and structures,leading to the transformation of its biogeochemical functions [21].Dissolved organic carbon concentration,aromaticity,and molecular weight (MW) of DOM have been shown to decrease after light irradiation [6],and photochemically reactive intermediates (PRIs), including triple excited state DOM $( ^ { 3 } \mathrm { { D O M ^ { * } } ) }$ ,singlet oxygen $( ^ { 1 } 0 _ { 2 } )$ ,and hydroxyl radical (·OH) [62] can be also produced,which accelerate the degradation of organic micropollutants [45,56].Generally,the origins and characteristics of DOM have profound effects on the photodegradationof DOM [13]. Forexample, the aromaticand methyl-containing substances in DOM were preferentially degraded [66].The production efficiency of $^ 1 0 _ { 2 }$ was highly related to the chemical structure of DOM(e.g., carbonyl,aromatic ketone,quinone groups,and condensed polycyclic aromatics）[71]．Photoconversion of dissolved black carbon converted the aromatic and methyl fractions to carboxyl, ester,quinone functional groups,and produced ${ } ^ { 3 } \mathrm { D O M ^ { \ast } }$ and $^ { 1 } \dot { 0 } _ { 2 } ,$ whereas the generation of-OH was not observed [14].While the photoconversion of pyrochar derived DOM was found to convert the condensed aromatics to aliphatic,protein, tannins and yield a considerable quantity of·OH and $^ 1 0 _ { 2 }$ [66].However,HDOM has significant heterogeneity in components and properties,such as higher molecular weight, content of aliphatic compounds and organic functional groups, and lower content of aromatic and aromatic compounds [45,48]. Therefore,the photo-transformation of HDOM may be significantly different. Yet the photo-transformation of HDOM remains seldom systematically reported.Besides,the preparation temperature of hydrochar is one of the key factors affecting the properties of HDOM [26,48]. However,it remains unclear if the variations in HDOM characteristics that depend on carbonization temperature account for the differences in photochemical transformation behavior.

Spectroscopy,mass spectrometry,and chromatography have been widely used to analyze and characterize changes in fluorescent and nonfluorescent substances,molecular compositions,and PRIs during the photoconversion of DOM.Previous studies have explored the changes in fluorescent and non-fluorescent substances by multispectral techniques, includingultraviolet-visible(UV-Vis)， excitation-emission matrix (EEM),synchronous fluorescence (SF),and Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) spectra [12,64,65]. Fourier transform ion cyclotron resonance mass spectrometry (FT-ICR MS) has appeared as an analytic technique in recent years for studying the photochemical behavior of DOM at the molecular level [51].In addition to the changes of fluorescent and non-fluorescent components,the photo-transformation of DOM is accompanied by the formation of PRIs,which can be elucidated using molecular probes [62].Recently, the combination of SF and FTIR with general two-dimensional correlation spectroscopy (2D-COs) can provide further sequential order of fluorescent and non-fluorescent components in HDOM during the photo-transformation [24,74]. However, the general 2D-COS cannot elucidate the effect of external perturbation variable (irradiation time) on the structural changes.Fortunately,the emergence of moving window 2D-COS will provide a promising strategy to reveal the structural changes of DOM along external perturbation [20, 61].To the best of the authors'knowledge,there are no studies that combine general 2D-COS and moving-window 2D-COS to analyze the photo-transformation of DOM.Consequently,the combined utilization of the techniques and analytical methods mentioned above can provide a detailed and insightful understanding of photo-transformation of HDOM.

Alternanthera philoxeroides,a typical invasive plant in China,proliferates in freshwater lakes and rivers during the summer and seriously endangers aquatic ecosystem 6O].Therefore,A. philoxeroides was subjected to hydrothermal carbonizationat18o,220,and $2 6 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } ,$ followed by extraction of DOM,and subjected to photochemical irradiation in this study.The main objectives of this study were to:(1) investigate changes in the composition and structure during photo-transformation of HDOM using UV-Vis,EEM,SF,FTIR,and FT-ICR MS;(2) combine SFand FTIR with general 2D-COS and moving window 2D-COS to obtain new insight into the response order and response time changes in fluorescent and non-fluorescent components during the photoconversion of HDOM; and (3) explore the heterogeneities in the production of PRIs from HDOM. The obtained results will be beneficial for providing an insightful and detailed understanding of the photo-transformation mechanisms of HDOM produced at different temperatures and for predicting the possible effects and processesof hydrochar before large-scale application.

# 2．Materials and methods

# 2.1．Material preparation and DOM extraction

A. philoxeroides residues were homogenously mixed with ultrapure Milli-Q water to achieve a solid-liquid ratio of 1:10 $\mathrm { ( m / v ) }$ and hydrothermally carbonized in a stainless-steel autoclave.The temperature was raised at a rate of $5 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } / \mathrm { m i n }$ to reach the preset temperatures of 180, 220, and $2 6 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ (according the practical application reported in previous studies）[10,65]，then held for $^ \mathrm { ~ 2 ~ h ~ }$ before cooling naturally.The carbonized products were filtered through $0 . 4 5 ~ { \mu \mathrm { m } }$ cellulose acetate membranes,and the solids on the membrane were collected,dried at $1 0 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ to a constant weight,and ground through a loo-mesh sieve to obtain the hydrochar.

DOM extraction was achieved by mixing A.philoxeroides hydrochar with ultrapure Milli-Q water to achieve a solid-liquid ratio of 1:20 $( \mathtt { m } /$ v),extracting it in a shaker at $2 5 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ and $1 8 0 ~ \mathrm { r p m }$ for $2 4 \ \mathrm { h }$ according to the reported results [17,26],and filtering through $0 . 4 5 ~ { \mu \mathrm { m } }$ cellulose acetate membranes to obtain HDOMs (HDOM180,HDOM220,and HDOM260 representing DOM products derived from hydrochar produced at 180, 220,and $2 6 0 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C } ,$ ,respectively).

# 2.2.Photochemical irradiation

Previous study has shown that the fraction of DOM-absorbed photons decreases monotonically from $9 2 . 1 \pm 7 . 7 \%$ at $2 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ to $5 . 0 \pm 3 . 6 \%$ at $7 2 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ [59],and the photochemical behavior of DOM is mainly triggered by ultraviolet (UV) radiation in sunlight,especially in the wavelength range of $2 8 0 { - } 4 0 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ [19,37]. Therefore,in this study,a $5 0 0 ~ \mathrm { W }$ mercury lamp equipped with a $4 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ filter was used to simulate UV irradiation in solar,and the spectral irradiance of the lamp surface is shown in Fig.S1,the iradiation intensity was measured to be approximately $2 . 3 \ \mathrm { W / m } ^ { 2 }$ ,which was close to that on the land surface on a midsummer day in the plains of China [1,5O,55].All photochemical reactions were performed in a photochemical reactor(GHX-II Shanghai Jiapeng).To minimize light shielding during photochemical transformation and internal filtering efect during fluorescence spectroscopy measurement, the initial concentration of HDOM was set at $1 0 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { C / L }$ (measured as total organic carbon)[8,33],and the irradiation volume was $5 0 \mathrm { m L }$ .Samples were withdrawnat 0,0.5,1,2,4,6,8,and $1 2 \mathrm { h }$ after initiation of irradiation for DOC,UV-Vis,EEM,and SF analyses.The remaining samples were freeze-dried and used for FTIR analysis.Benzene,furfuryl alcohol (FFA),and 2,4,6-trimethylphenol (TMP） were selected as molecular probes to detect·OH, $^ 1 0 _ { 2 } ,$ and ${ } ^ { 3 } \mathrm { D O M ^ { \ast } }$ ,respectively [44].According to the results of the preliminary experiment, the initial concentrations of benzene,FFA and TMP were set as O.2,1.6, $1 . 6 ~ \mathrm { m M }$ respectively.The·OH was quantified by detecting the amount of phenol formed,whereas $^ 1 0 _ { 2 }$ and ${ } ^ { 3 } \mathrm { D O M ^ { \ast } }$ were quantified by the reduction of FFA and TMP,respectively.A detailed determination of the generation efficiencies of PRIs is given in Text S1.The probe compounds were analyzed using high-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC, Shimadzu LC-2oA)，and chromatographic parameters are shown in Table S1.

# 2.3.Spectral measurements

The absorption spectra were measured using a UV-Vis spectrophotometer (Shimadzu UV-27oo) with a wavelength range and interval of $2 0 0 { - } 8 0 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ and $1 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ,respectively.The EEM spectra were measured using a HORIBA fluorologic-3 fluorescence spectrometer with a CCD detector and a 45o W xenon lamp.Excitation and emission spectral ranges were set to $2 5 0 { \mathrm { - } } 4 5 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ $5 \mathrm { n m }$ interval) and $2 5 0 { - } 6 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ (1 nm interval),respectively.The internal filtering effect was corrected using the drEEM toolbox and Rayleigh scattering.Parallel factor analysis (PARAFAC) of the HDOMs was performed using the drEEM and N-way toolboxes in MATLAB R2012a [4O],and a total dataset of 72 samples was modeled, which met the requirements of the analysis methodology [49].Synchronous fluorescence spectroscopy was measured using the same fluorescence spectrometer,but with a PMT detector.The excitation wavelength range was set to $2 5 0 \mathrm { - } 5 5 0 \mathrm { n m }$ with a constant offset $\Delta \lambda$ $= 6 0 \mathrm { n m } )$ [23].The FTIR spectra of freeze-dried samples were obtained using a Nicolet iS50 FTIR spectrometer with 32 scans acquired at $2 \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ resolution in the range of $4 0 0 0 ~ \mathrm { { c m } ^ { - 1 } }$ to $4 0 0 ~ \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ .Detailed calculation procedures on the spectral parameters of UV-Vis and EEM spectra (E2:E3 (absorption coefficients ratio), $\mathrm { S U V A } _ { 2 5 4 }$ (specific UV absorption of DOC at $2 5 4 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ ),HIX (humification index),BIX (biogenic index)) are provided in Text S2.To further analyze the differences in the molecular change characteristics (response order and response time) of fluorescent and non-fluorescent components during photoconversion of HDOMs, general and moving window 2D-COS analysis of SF and FTIR spectra were performed using“2D Shige"software [8,74].The window size was setas 3 $( 2 \mathbf { m } + 1 )$ .Detailed information on the 2D-COS analysis method is available in Text S3.

The samples used for FT-ICR MS determination were prepared and measured under the same conditions,which were desalted and enriched by solid phase extraction before detection [62]; the detailed description isavailable in Text S4.HDOM samples were analyzed bya Bruker SolariX FT-ICR MS equipped with a $1 5 . 0 \mathrm { T }$ superconducting magnet and negative mode ESI.Detailed test parameters and information on the method of screening data, the delineation of molecular species,and the calculation of corresponding indexes (e.g.，double bond equivalents (DBE)and modified aromaticity index(AImod)) are described in Text S5 and Text S6.

# 3．Results and discussion

# 3.1．EEM-PARAFAC of HDOMs during photolysis

# 3.1.1.HDOM components identified by EEM-PARAFAC

The three fluorescent components of HDOMs separated using the PARAFAC model were compared with published data from the OpenFluor database [39] (Fig.1).C1 $( \mathrm { E x } / \mathrm { E m } = 2 4 0 ( 3 6 0 ) / 4 6 0 \ \mathrm { n m } )$ was identified as a humic-like substance associated with highly degraded, high MW,and aromatic DOM,which was widely present in aquatic ecosystems [53].C2 $( \mathrm { E x } / \mathrm { E m } = 3 2 5 / 3 9 0 ~ \mathrm { n m } )$ ）was a humus-like substance with relatively low excitation and emission,and consisted of relatively low MW and potentially labile components [53].In previous study，C2 was shown to be involved in the photochemical transformation of DOM[38].C3 $( \mathrm { E x } / \mathrm { E m } = 2 8 0 / 3 3 0 \ \mathrm { n m } )$ was a protein-like component in HDOMs,similar to tryptophan，which had high bioavailability [28].

# 3.1.2. Variations in HDOM spectra and their spectral parameters

The trends in the EEM spectra of HDOMs under UV irradiation were shown in Fig.S2a.The initial total fluorescence intensities of HDOM180, HDOM220,and HDOM260 were 6.02,7.20,and 7.41 RU,respectively. The initial total fluorescence intensities of HDOMs increased with the increase in hydrothermal carbonization temperature,which may be owing to the fact that the higher temperature in the hydrothermal carbonization process promoted increased biomass pyrolysis,resulting in a higher degree of DOM release in hydrochar.In contrast, C1 release decreased with increasing carbonization temperature,which may be owing to the heterogeneities of the decomposition temperatures of different biological macromolecules [18,72].In addition, the fluorescence intensity decreased significantly after $^ { 1 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ of UV irradiation $( P < 0 . 0 1 )$ ),indicating thatHDOMis degraded.The degradation rates of the fluorescence intensities of C1,C2,and C3 were significantly different during irradiation $( P < 0 . 0 1 )$ ,withdegradationrateof $0 . 0 7 7 \pm 0 . 0 9$ $0 . 1 3 2 \pm 0 . 0 1 4$ and $0 . 1 6 4 \pm 0 . 0 1 5 \mathrm { h } ^ { - 1 }$ for HDOM180, $0 . 0 7 4 \pm 0 . 0 1 3$ $0 . 1 3 4 \pm 0 . 0 1 8$ ，and $0 . 1 6 6 \pm 0 . 0 2 6 \mathrm { { h } ^ { - 1 } }$ for HDOM220,and 0.074 $\pm \ : 0 . 0 7$ ， $0 . 1 5 3 \pm 0 . 0 1 4$ ，and $\mathbf { 0 . 1 8 7 \pm 0 . 0 2 \ h ^ { - 1 } }$ for HDOM260,respectively (Fig.2). It was worth noting that the degradation rates of C2 and C3 increased with the increase in carbonization temperature,whereas C1 showed the opposite trend.In addition,because the degradation rates of C2 and C3 were significantly higher than that of C1,the fluorescence ratios were significantly lower than that of C1 after $^ { 1 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ of UV irradiation (Fig. S2b),indicating that C2 and C3 had stronger photodegradation potential than C1.Previous studies have reported that DOM with low molecular weight has a strong photolysis performance [65], and protein-like substances in DOMcan undergo photoconversion under light irradiation [4,5],which is consistent with the results of this study.

The moving window 2D-COS analysis was performed to visualize the distinct spectral intensity changes in a specific region with increasing irradiation time for explicit interpretation.UV-induced moving window 2D-COS of SF spectra of HDOM180,HDOM220 and HDOM260 were exhibited in Fig.6a,b,c,respectively.Moving window 2D-COS analysis of SF spectra showed that three common cross peaks appeared at 258, 285 and $3 3 8 \mathrm { n m }$ at $^ { 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ and HDOM180 exhibited a unique cross peak at ${ 2 8 5 } \mathrm { n m }$ at $^ { 8 \mathrm { h } }$ .However,the peak intensity and peak coverage of HDOM180 at ${ 2 8 5 } \mathrm { n m }$ at 8 h was higher than that at ${ 2 8 5 } \mathrm { n m }$ at $^ { 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ ,while the peak intensity and peak coverage at 258 and $2 8 5 \mathrm { n m }$ at 2 h showed a trend of $\mathrm { H D O M 2 6 0 } > \mathrm { H D O M 2 2 0 } > \mathrm { H D O M 1 } 8 0$ 、Furthermore,the cross peak at $3 2 0 \mathrm { n m }$ of HDOM180 changed from 4 h to $^ { 8 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ of HDOM220 and HDOM260,indicating that the protein-like substances in HDOM were more sensitive to low irradiation time and the photoresponse time of humic-like substances was relatively lagging as the carbonation temperature increased.

# 3.3.Molecular composition transformation during photolysis of HDOMs based on FT-ICRMS

To better elucidate the conversion of HDOMs during phototransformation, FT-ICR MS was used to analyze the photoconversion process at the molecular level. The results showed a significant production of carbohydrates,tannins,unsaturated hydrocarbons,and condensed aromatic molecules after $^ { 1 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ of irradiation (Fig. 7),and a significant change in the total molecular number,weighted average molecular weight $( \mathrm { { M W _ { w a } } ) }$ ， ${ \mathrm { O / C } } _ { \mathrm { w a } } ,$ and weighted average double bond equivalent $\left( \mathrm { D B E } _ { \mathrm { w a } } \right)$ (Table 1), suggesting that molecules with high MW were produced during the photoconversion of HDOM，which was consistent with the results of E2:E3 (Fig.S3d).Previous studies have shown that the MW and number of DOM molecules tend to decrease after light irradiation [21,74],however, Liu etal.,[31] reported that the $m / z$ of dissolved organic carbon leached by compost increased after photo-bleaching and Waggoner et al.，[54] also found that hydroxyl radical initiated oxidation of lignin to produce black carbon-like condensed aromatic compounds,which may be similar to the addition of oxygen to the double bond (oxidation) process.HDOMs exhibited the same phenomenon,most likely owing to the PRIs-mediated photoflocculation of HDOMs,generating photopolymers of larger molecular size [22]. The values of $\mathrm { { O } / \mathrm { { C _ { w a } } } }$ and $\mathrm { D B E } _ { \mathrm { w a } }$ in HDOM220 and HDOM260 increased after UV irradiation,indicating that the oxidation and unsaturation of HDOM molecules increased.After $^ { 1 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ of irradiation,the total content of lignins and lipids in HDOM180,HDOM220,and HDOM260 decreased from $8 2 \%$ to $7 2 \%$ ，83to $7 3 \%$ ,and 83 to $7 5 \%$ respectively (Fig.S5). In addition, the relative abundance plots of the disappeared and produced molecules show that lignins were the most active components involved in molecular conversion regardless of the HDOMtype(Figs. 8 and S6). Thiswassimilartothe photo-transformation of DOM in aquatic ecosystems,in which the lignin component of DOM was more sensitive to light irradiation [41,52].

As shown in Fig.S7,CHO and CHON molecules were the major constituent molecules in HDOMs.After irradiation, the percentage of CHO molecules decreased significantly,whereas the percentages of N and S-containing molecules increased significantly.Furthermore,it could be concluded from Figs.S6 and S8 that the proportion of N and Scontaining molecules in the disappeared molecules was significantly higher than that in the CHO molecules,and the produced molecules contained a larger proportion of CHON molecules.This suggested that N and S-containing molecules were more susceptible to UV irradiation than the CHO molecules.Furthermore,Fig.S9 showed that the relative abundance of the generated molecular formulas with high mass-tocharge ratios were higher than the disappeared molecular formulas, suggesting that molecules with higher MW were generated.In addition, molecules with $\mathrm { D B E } = 2 0 $ appeared in the produced molecules in Fig.SlO and their relative abundance were higher than that of disappeared molecules,suggesting that N and S-containing molecules produced some molecules with higher DBE after UV irradiation,which was consistent with previous studies [21,74].

# 3.5.Possble effects of photochemical behavior of HDOM on hazardous materials in aquatic ecosystems

The photochemical behavior of DOM will significantly affect the interaction of DOMwith exogenous hazardous materials (heavy metals and organic pollutants).He et al.,[18] found that photoirradiation can decrease the binding capacity of DOM with heavy metals.While heavy metals in metal-DOM complexes can undergo photoreduction by direct photolysis and photochemically generated PRIs [11],which will affect the morphology of heavy metals in the environment.Furthermore,DOM can play both promotional and inhibitory roles during the photodegradation of organic pollutants in aquatic ecosystems [69].On one hand, the PRIs generated during the photo-transformation of DOM itself can accelerate the photodegradation of organic pollutants [29].On the other hand, intermediates formed by the reaction of organic pollutants with PRIs can be converted back to the parent compound by accepting electrons from the antioxidant groups (phenols) present in the DOM [57, 58].Therefore,the photochemical behavior of HDOM will significantly affect the fate and bioavailability of exogenous hazardous materials (heavy metals and organic pollutants) in aquatic ecosystems.

# 4.Conclusion

FT-ICR MS,HPLC,and EEM-PARAFAC combined with general and moving-window 2D-COS of FTIR and SF were used to investigate the differences in the photochemical behavior of HDOM produced at different carbonization temperature.As the hydrothermal temperature increased,the degradation rates of C2 and C3 increased,and the degradation rate of C3 was significantly higher than those of C2 and C1. The 2D-COS results showed that with increasing carbonization temperature,the photoresponse in HDOM changed from the preferential photolysis of phenolic structures of humic-like substances in HDOM180 to the preferential photolysis of aliphatic and amide II of protein-like substances in HDOM220 and HDOM260,the fluorescent component of HDOM was more sensitive to low irradiation times,while the duration of the photochemical behavior of the non-fluorescent component was prolonged.FT-ICR MS results showed that the $\mathrm { M W _ { w a } }$ and $\mathrm { D B E } _ { \mathrm { w a } }$ of HDOM molecules were increased after irradiation,and N and S-containing molecules,including lignins and lipids were more sensitive to UV irradiation.In addition，the photo-transformation of HDOMswas accompanied by the generation of PRIs $\mathrm { \Gamma ^ { 3 } D O M ^ { \ast } }$ and $^ 1 0 _ { 2 } )$ ， showing a trend of $\mathrm { H D O M 2 6 0 } > \mathrm { H D O M 2 2 0 } > \mathrm { H D O M 1 8 0 }$ .In contrast, there was no significant generation of-OH.These findings will be beneficial for guiding the application of hydrochar and understanding the potential environmental behaviors and risks of HDOM to in aquatic ecosystems.

# Environmental implication

Hydrochar-derived dissolved organic matter (HDOM) is a potential source of DOM pool that can undergo photo-transformation and may further influence the fates,bio-availabilities,and toxicities of exogenous hazardous species in aquatic ecosystems.The differences in the molecular change characteristics (response order and response time) of fluorescent and non-fluorescent components,the changes in molecular compositions,and the formation of photochemically reactive intermediates of HDOMwere revealed at the molecular level in this study. Our study will establish a detailed and insightful understanding of the photochemical behavior that may occur after large-scale application of hydrochar.
