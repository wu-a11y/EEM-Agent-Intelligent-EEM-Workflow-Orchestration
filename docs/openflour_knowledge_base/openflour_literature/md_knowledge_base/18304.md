Microplastics were an extensively detected pollutant in the environment, but microplastic-derived dissolved organic matter (MP-DOM) has received less atention,much lessits impact on the binding behavior of pollutants (e.g.,pharmaceuticals).In this study,DOM derived from two typical MPs,i.e.,polyethylene terephthalate (PET) and polystyrene (PS) was generated by UV iradiation (a widely used way for MPs'aging treatment) and characterized by multiple spectroscopic techniques and methods. Chloramphenicol (CAP) and carbamazepine (CBZ) were selected to investigate the binding mechanism between MP-DOM and pharmaceuticals.After UV irradiation,the concentration of the disolved organic carbon,colored DOM,and carboxyl/carbonyl groups of MP-DOM increased. Moreover,the humic-like substance released preceding and more under UV irradiation. Furthermore, the protein-like substances on PET-DOMand the humic-like substances on PS-DOM were positively correlated to the binding capacity to the pharmaceuticals,respectively.2D-COS results revealed that the fluorescent materials having more oxygen-containing functional groups for MP-DOM preferentially interacted with the pharmaceuticals.Overal,the higher fluorescence quenching was related to the protein-like substance, CBZ,and PET-DOM as compared to the humic-like substance,CAP,and PS-DOM.It was verifiedby the relatively high binding ability $( \mathrm { l o g } K _ { \mathrm { M } } )$ for them (the protein-like substance: 5.15;CBZ: 4.6l; PET:4.48).This study first proved the environmental reactivityof MP-DOM to thepharmaceuticals highlighting the significance of the spectral properties for the binding behavior of MP-DOM with pharmaceuticals and the competitive sorption role of MP-DOM to the pollutants in the natural environment.

# 1．Introduction

Owing to the easy-to-shape feature and low price,plastics are widely used in our daily lives (Jehanno et al., 2O22). Meanwhile, the microplastics (MPs,particle size lower than $5 \mathrm { m m }$ ）areinevitablydischarged into the environment,which could directly originate from plastic products (e.g.,textiles,medicines,cosmetics,and detergents,etc.) or indirectly from the fragmentation of big plastic(Andrady，2O17).During the weathering (Bhagwat et al.，2021)，aging (Ding et al.，202O),and biodegradation (Miri et al.,2022) of MPs,MP-derived dissolved organic matter (MP-DOM) was generated.MPs can release dissolved organic carbon (DOC) when in contact with water for extended periods (Lin and Su,2022).If these organic carbons are bioavailable, these plastic leachates might stimulate microbial carbon fluxes in the ocean,especially in the surface layer (Romera-Castillo et al., 2Ol8). It has been reported that up to 23,6oo tons of MP-DOM annually are released by marine plastic litter which might make up to $1 0 ~ \%$ of DOM for the top $4 0 ~ { \mu \mathrm { m } }$ surface microlayer in some highly contaminated areas (Romera-Castillo et al., 2018).In addition,although few,it has been proven that MP-DOM could pose toxicity to human beings (colonic adenocarcinoma cells) (Xu et al., 2022).Despite the numerous reports that have investigated the properties and fate of MPs in the environment, there is a paucity of information on the characteristics and environmental impact of MP-DOM.Hereinto,it is crucial to characterize the properties of MP-DOM above all,which will help to understand the environmental influence connected with MPs.

Once entering the environment,MPs will undergo various aging processes (Jahnke et al., 2Ol7).Wherein, UV irradiation is identified as a strong driver for MP-DOM production (Potthoff et al.,2017).In fact, UV irradiation was a widely used way for MPs' aging treatment in prior studies (Zhu et al., 202O).After aging, the monomers,oligomers,and additives such as bisphenolA(BPA) and di-(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate of MPs were released to the aquatic media and formed the MP-DOM (Lee et al.,2020a; Lee et al., 202Ob). These complex compositions give MPDOM various functional groups and special properties such as lower molecular weight,higher protein substance,and weaker aromaticity (Chen et al.，2022b; Lee et al.,2021b). For example,the leaching property of BPA and the high protein-like fluorescence of DOM leached from BPA indicated that this fluorophore could be used as a proxy indicator of DOM leached from BPA-containing MPs (Lee et al., 2020a). But in fact, the fluorescence properties and the functional groups of MPDOM are similar to those of the natural DOM (NOM) (Lee and Hur, 2020; Lee et al., 2O2Ob). It means that MP-DOM might behave in interaction with the other pollutants and whether these interactions were similar to the NOMis unknown,in particular for the organic pollutants.

In recent years,pharmaceuticals have received much concern due to their persistence,potential environmental risks,and even genotoxicity to public health (Sanganyado et al., 20l7; Wilkinson et al., 20l7). As the representatives of pharmaceuticals,chloramphenicol (CAP) and carbamazepine (CBZ) have been widely detected in the environment with concentrations ranging from ng $\mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ to $\mu \mathbf { g } \ \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ due to the low removal efficiency in wastewater treatment plants (Almeida et al., 2021b; Nguyen et al.,2022).Moreover,a high level of CAP in water could damage cells and inhibit the growth of organisms (Xiong et al., 2019) and at the environmental concentration, CBZ might change the biological responses in marine bivalves (Almeida et al., 202la). Consequently, understanding their environmental behavior is essential for pharmaceutical pollution prevention and control.

Prior studies have shown that both MPs and DOMcould interact with pharmaceuticals and then impact the migration and transformation of pharmaceuticals in the environment (Ding et al., 2O22; Huang et al., 2022; Li et al., 2018; Nie et al., 2018). Among them, the binding of pharmaceuticals to DOM is a very important process that could be affected by the physicochemical properties of the compounds (i.e.,hydrophobicity and functional groups) (Rizzuto et al., 2O2la; Yan et al., 2015)and the DOM(i.e.,fluorescence components,molecular size,and concentration) (Huang et al., 2022; Yan etal., 2015).Furthermore, the complexes were readily changed by altering the molecular configuration of the compound and/or DOM with the changing ambient conditions (i. e., pH and ionic strength) (Maoz and Chefetz, 201O; Yan et al., 2015). Hence,the impact of these binding behaviors on the migration of pharmaceuticals could not be obtained easily.As previously mentioned, MP-DOM has similar properties to the NOM, illustrating that the binding between pharmaceuticals and MP-DOM could occur in theory as strongly as the NOM.It has been shown that both the protein-like (MPderived) and humic-like (MP photo-induced) exhibited fluorescence quenching with the copper concentration increased and the binding constants to the MP-DOM were comparable with those of the NOM, especially under UV irradiation conditions (Lee et al., 2O2la). However, it is worth noting that the knowledge about the binding mechanism between MP-DOM and pharmaceuticals is much scarcer so far.

In order to characterize DOM and explore the binding mechanism between contaminants and DOM,multiple analytical techniques and methodologies have been introduced in the relevant studies,including UV-visible absorption (UV-vis),three-dimensional excitation-emission matrix (3DEEM) fluorescence spectroscopy combined parallel factor analysis (PARAFAC),and synchronous fluorescence spectra (SFS) combined two-dimensional correlation spectroscopy (2DCOs) (Yan et al., 2018; Yan et al., 2023),etc. 3DEEM-PARAFAC is extensively utilized to determine and quantify the distributions of the fluorophores in DOM (Coble, 1996; Stedmon and Bro, 2008). 2DCOS often used UV-vis, SFS, and Fourier transform infrared spectrum (FTIR)as the probes,which were capable of resolving peak overlapping problems and providing information about the sequential orders of the spectral changes caused by the external disturbances (Chen et al.,2O15).These different and complementary spectral signals could help to better describe the binding behavior between pharmaceuticals and MP-DOM.

In the present study,two types of MPs,polyethylene terephthalate (PET)and polystyrene (PS) were selected and aged under UV irradiation treatment to simulate the leach of MP-DOM. CAP and CBZ were chosen as target pharmaceuticals.A dialysis equilibrium experiment was introduced to investigate the binding capacity between the different sizes of MP-DOM and the two drugs.Fluorescence quenching titration combines multiple spectroscopic techniques and methods were applied to simulate and analyze the binding mechanism between the fluorophores in MP-DOM and the two drugs.Considering almost no study has addressed the binding mechanism between MP-DOM and pharmaceuticals,this study specifically aimed to (1) characterize MPs and the leached MP-DOM with and without UV irradiation;(2) explore the binding capacity of the different sizes of MP-DOM towards the two drugs; (3) elucidate the binding mechanism between the fluorescence components of MP-DOM and the pharmaceuticals including the binding site,substance,sequence,and affinity.

# 2.2.MPs aging and MP-DOM preparation

In the aging test, $6 0 g$ of MP samples were added into $3 0 0 \mathrm { m L }$ Milli-Q water under magnetic stirring with a $5 0 0 ~ \mathrm { m L }$ quartz conical flask. Because the main purpose of this study was to characterize the MP-DOM and its effect on the binding properties of MP-DOMand the drugs,a high level of MP was selected to obtain enough MP-DOM samples within a short time.A15 W UV lamp $( \lambda = 2 5 4 ~ \mathrm { n m } )$ was used to irradiate MPs at ${ } ^ { 2 5 } \ { } ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ room temperature (Huang et al., 2O21). # 2.6. Data analysis

# 2.6.1.2D-COS

2D-COS provides an in-depth understanding of a variety of spectral data from various experiments obtained under the influence of applied external perturbations (Noda, 2Oo6).This method can serve to uncover the sequence of the influence of external factors on different DOM components.The details of the 2D-COS analysis theory have been explained in the literature (Noda,199O; Noda and Ozaki, 2004). In this study,SFS of MP-DOM from the MPs aging process,the dialysis experiment,and fluorescence quenching titration were applied for 2D-COs analysis by 2D Shige software (Kansai Prefectural University, Japan), as well as FTIR spectra of MP-DOM from the fluorescence quenching titration.Aging time,dialysis equilibrium time,and pharmaceutical concentrations were used as the external perturbations.

# 3．Results and discussion

# 3.1．Characterizations of virgin and aged MPs

The different surface morphology of the pristine and aged MPs is shown in Fig.S1.After aging,both PET and PS are rough on the surface and prone to disintegration and embrittlement compared to virgin MPs with a smooth surface.These changes were especially more obvious for PS that a smaller size distribution of PS particles was observed (Fig. S1f). These observations have been found in many public researches (Liu et al.,2019; Liu et al., 2021). Besides, the surface color of MPs changed from white to yellow due to the fragmentation of MPs and the generation of the chromophores,which were compared with those in the previous studies (Fig. S1b) (Priyanka and Saravanakumar, 2022; Song et al., 2017).

FTIR spectra showed that the adsorption characteristic peak positions before and after aging were not affected,but the peak values were higher for the aged ${ \tt M P s }$ ,and the absorption peaks for PETand PS were different (Fig.2a).In detail, the absorption band at 3435 and $3 4 5 0 \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ for PET and PS respectively indicates the presence of the -OH group, whose intensities were deeper after aging.The peak at $1 7 1 0 ~ \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ and $1 2 5 0 ~ \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ for PET corresponds to the stretching vibration of the $\scriptstyle \mathrm { C = } 0$ bond and in-plane $\mathsf { C } { \mathrm { - } } \mathsf { C }$ of the benzene ring (Priyanka and Saravanakumar, 2022).While peaks at 1030,1450,1490,and $1 6 0 0 ~ \mathrm { { c m } ^ { - 1 } }$ for PS were inferred as the deformation and skeleton vibration of C一O, $\mathrm { C - H }$ and $\scriptstyle \mathrm { C = } 0$ (Chia et al., 2012; Mao et al., 2020).

Furthermore,XPS was conducted to further verify the changes in the surface chemical compositions and functional groups before and after the aging of MPs.In addition to the $\scriptstyle \mathrm { C - C / C = C }$ ,some O-containing groups were still found in the original MPs (Fig.2b).It was found that the percentage of the peak at $2 8 6 . 5 \ \mathrm { e V }$ for $\mathrm { c } { - } 0$ increased significantly in the aged MPs,in particular for PS.However,the changes of $\scriptstyle \mathtt { C } = 0$ peaked at nearly $2 8 7 ~ \mathrm { e V }$ and ${ \bf O - C = O }$ peaked at $2 8 9 . 2 \ \mathrm { e V }$ were not observed in this study which might be attributed to the short aging time (Herath and Salehi，2022； Mao et al.，2020).With the more oxygen-containing groups generated from the UV-aging process,the leaching of DOM from the aged MPs was strongly accelerated (Zhang et al., 2022).

# 3.2. Characterization of the leaching MP-DOM

The properties of MP-DOM were determined by FTIR,UV-vis,2DSFS-COS,and EEM-PARAFAC. Compared with the original and aged MPs,the MP-DOM exhibited totally different FTIR spectra (Fig.S2a),in particular for PET-DOM,which had been confirmed in the study of PE-, PP-,and PVC-DOM (Lee and Hur, 2020; Lee et al., 2020b). After 15 days of UV aging,similar peaks both observed in the two MP-DOMs.Wherein, the peaks near 1400,1550,and $1 6 6 0 ~ \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ are related to the $- \mathrm { C H } _ { 3 } ,$ $\mathrm { C O O ^ { - } }$ and $\scriptstyle \mathrm { C = } 0$ stretching,which are the typical functionalities of monomers and additives released from the corresponding MPs (Chen et al., 2022b; Lee and Hur, 2020; Mao et al., 2020).

The UV-vis absorption spectra of MP-DOM with the increasing aging time are shown in Fig.S2b,as well as the typical UV-vis parameters (Fig.S2c).A shoulder range from 220 to $2 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ was observed and increased with the aging time for PET-DOM,which was related to the n$\pi ^ { * }$ electronic transition of $\scriptstyle { \mathsf { C } } = 0$ from carboxylic acid or ketone (Chen et al.,2022c).However, this shoulder was not found for PS-DOM.With the increasing aging time,the DOC concentration showed an increased trend.A similar profile in the DOC concentration increased during UV irradiation was observed in many previous studies (Lee et al., 2O20a; Xu et al., 2O22),which could significantly influence the DOC concentration in the surrounding environment (Chen et al., 2022a; Liu et al., 2017). Besides,the UV-vis parameters are in accordance with the DOC concentration (Xu et al., 2022). Similarly, the values of α254 $, E _ { 2 5 3 } / E _ { 2 0 3 } ,$ and S275-295 increased with the aging time in this study,demonstrating that the concentration of the CDOM and the ratio of the aliphatic chain on the benzene ring oxidized into carboxyl/carbonyl groups increased, but the molecular weight of DOM decreased with the aging process.With increasing aging time,the released MP-DOMunderwent both promotion and degradation processes with the promotion effect stronger than the degradation effect,which resulted in increased DOC and CDOM concentration (Lee et al., 2O2Ob).Additionally, the phenomenon that the carboxyl/carbonyl groups of MP-DOM increased after UVaging was also observed for the DOM released from the other MPs (such as polyvinyl chloride and polypropylene),and these oxidized groups were identified to be related to the humic substances (Zhou et al., 2022).Whereas Ren et al. (2023) reported that $\scriptstyle \mathrm { C } = \mathrm { O }$ was found to connect both the proteinand the humic-like substance in two aging conditions and the transformation of the functional groups during the aging process might vary from the different fluorophores,aging treatments,and/or MP types (Ren et al.,2O23).Hence,this aspect requires more attention.

To obtain the sequential changes in the fluorescence components of MP-DOM with the increasing aging time,synchronous and asynchronous maps of 2DCOS were drawn with SFS (Fig.3a).In the synchronous map of PET (Table S3),three auto peaks were observed at 232,282,and $3 3 3 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ along the diagonal line,which represents the tyrosine-,the tryptophan-,and the fulvic-like substance,respectively.For PS,three similar auto peaks were peaking at 232,270,and $3 1 0 \mathrm { n m }$ ,revealing that similar fluorescence substances were produced after aging.Additionally, the highest fluorescence intensity of the tryptophan-like was both found in PET and PS samples demonstrating that this fluorophore was more susceptible under UV irradiation. In the asynchronous map of PET, four negative cross-peaks were displayed at 232/215,282/215,333/215, and $3 3 3 / 2 8 2 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ,and two positive cross-peaks were observed at 370/ 282 and $3 7 0 / 3 3 3 { \mathrm { n m } } ; $ and for PS,three negative cross-peaks appeared at 270/232,310/232,and $3 1 0 / 2 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ .According to Noda's principle (Noda and Ozaki, 20o4), the sequence of the fluorescence components for MP-DOM derived from the aging process was ordered as $2 1 5 \ \mathrm { n m } >$ 232/282/333 nm and $3 7 0 \mathrm { n m } > 2 8 2 \mathrm { n m } > 3 3 3 \mathrm { n m }$ for PET,and $2 3 2 \mathrm { n m }$ $> 2 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m } > 3 1 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ for PS.It could be concluded that the tyrosine- and the humic-like substance released precedence with UV irradiation, followed by the tryptophan-and the fulvic-like substance.The feature of the fluorophores (i.e.，the humic-like substance) released sequence during the aging process which was partially in accordance with those of PET-DOM released under the Fenton process (the humic $>$ the protein) (Priyanka and Saravanakumar, 2O22). It suggested that the sequence of the released fluorescence substance might be distinct among different aging treatments and irrelevant to the types of MPs.This conclusion provided an authentic result helping to understand the character of MPDOM released from various aging treatments.

Since plastic additives such as BPA and di-(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate had a fluorophore similar to the protein/phenol-like component (Goncalves-Araujo et al.，2015),the protein-like component was supposed as the characteristic component in MP-DOM(Lee et al.,202Oa).The changes in the fluorescence intensity of Cl-C5 are shown in Fig.4c.With compared to the dark condition, the fluorescence intensity of C2 in PET-DOM strikingly increased with increasing aging time, followed by C1;while the counterpartin PS-DOM was C1 and C4.Combined with the results of 2DCOS,it illustrated that not only was the humic-like substance released faster than the proteinlike substance,but also the amount of fluorescence intensity for the former was higher than that for the latter.Whereas the dominant fluorophore in different aged MPs was distinguishing from each other.

# 3.3.Binding capacity of CAP and CBZ to MP-DOM

# 3.3.1.Changes in the spectral properties for diferent sizes of MP-DOM

In order to assess the binding capacity and influencing factor of the different sizes of MP-DOM towards CAP and CBZ, the $K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ value was calculated,and the different spectra (i.e.,UV absorbance,SFS,and 3DEEM) for the different sizes of MP-DOM was detected.After being separated by the dialysis,most of the small size of MP-DOM transferred to the outside of the bag with the absorbance of the $< 1$ kDa MP-DOM apparent increased,as well as the values of DOC,α254, $E _ { 2 5 3 } / E _ { 2 0 3 } ,$ and $ { S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 } }$ (excluding $E _ { 2 5 3 } / E _ { 2 0 3 }$ for PS) (Fig. S3).But the values of the corresponding parameters for $^ { > 1 }$ kDa MP-DOM were overwhelmingly much higher than those for $< 1$ kDa MP-DOM when reaching equilibrium (despite declining gently),revealing that the primary DOMwas related to the larger size of MP-DOM.

Moreover,2DCOS analysis displayed the isolated sequence of the fluorescence components in the $< 1$ kDa MP-DOM during the equilibrium process (Fig.3b).In the synchronous map (Table S5),two auto peaks were displayed at 208 and $2 8 2 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ for the PET system,which corresponded to the tyrosine- and the tryptophan-like substance;and there were three auto peaks shown at 205,270,and $3 3 3 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ for PS system,representing the tyrosine-,the tryptophan-,and the fulvic-like substance,respectively.In addition,the highest fluorescence intensity of the tryptophan-and the tyrosine-like substance was found for PET and PS,illustrating that the fluorescence of MP-DOM separate sensitivity through dialysis was higher for the protein-like substance.In the asynchronous map,one negative and positive cross-peak were shown at $2 8 2 /$ ${ 2 0 8 } \ \mathrm { n m }$ and $3 4 5 / 2 8 2 \ \mathrm { n m }$ for the PET system; and for PS,one negative cross-peak was displayed at $3 3 3 / 2 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ,and three positive cross-peak were located at 270/205,360/205,360/333 nm.Consequently,the relative sensitivity of the fluorescence components during the dialysis process was: $2 0 8 / 3 4 5 \mathrm { n m } > 2 8 2$ for PETand $2 7 0 / 3 6 0 \mathrm { n m } > 2 0 5 / 3 3 3 \mathrm { n m }$ for PS.These results suggested that the fluorophore transferred sequence of the two bulk MP-DOM during dialysis was different,although the sensitive fluorescence substances were similar.

Besides,the dominant fluorophores in the two MP-DOM( $\ l < 1$ kDa) were also different.With increasing dialysis equilibrium time,the fluorescence intensity of the fluorophore peaks in $< 1$ kDa MP-DOM dramatically increased (Fig. S4a).In the $< 1$ kDa PET-DOM solution, the fluorescence intensity of C4 and C5 significantly increased and was much higher than the other components; while in the $^ { < 1 }$ kDa of PS-DOM solution, the counterpart was Cl and C5 (Fig. S4b).It demonstrated that the protein-and the humic-like substance was the major fluorophore in the smaller size of PET-DOM and PS-DOM,respectively.That is,the different main fluorophores in MP-DOM released from different MPs could further impact the binding behavior between MP-DOM and the pollutants.

# 3.3.2.Binding behavior of CAP and CBZ to MP-DOM

In general, the concentration of CAP and CBZ increased rapidly within $^ \textrm { \scriptsize 4 h }$ and gradually reached stable after 8h (Fig.S5).After checking the equilibration across the membrane $( 4 8 \ \mathrm { h } )$ ,the pharmaceutical concentrations in and outside of the bag were both measured at the different initial CAP and CBZ concentrations.It found that a relatively high correlation was obtained for the different initial CAP concentration solution $( R > 0 . 9 9 )$ than CBZ $( R > 0 . 8 0 )$ ） (Fig.5a),which suggested that the binding behavior between MP-DOM and CAP was more similar than CBZ at different initial concentrations.Furthermore,a decreasing trend of the correlation was shown between CAP and CBZ with the initial concentration increased, indicating that the more similar binding behavior between the two drugs existed at the lower initial concentration.Given all the above results,these two MP-DOM might have higher binding capacity towards CAP and the binding sites decreased with the drug concentration increased.

In order to determine the binding capacity of MP-DOM, $K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ and $B _ { \mathrm { D O M } }$ were calculated which was shown in Table 1.Strikingly,CAP binding to PET-DOM had the lowest average $B _ { \mathrm { D O M } }$ value in different initial CAP/CBZ concentrations $( 8 0 . 7 0 ~ \% )$ ,while there was no substantial difference among the other binding combinations (PS-CAP: $9 3 . 0 2 ~ \%$ ：PET-CBZ:89.72; PS-CBZ: $9 0 . 8 3 ~ \%$ ).These results suggested that MP-DOM showed high binding capacity with the selected pharmaceuticals,and these values were within the range of other pharmaceuticals reported in the literature (Ding et al., 2O13; Pan et al., 2009).

The average $\scriptstyle \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ value for PET-CAP,PS-CAP,PET-CBZ,and PSCBZ was 1.01,1.08,1.14,and $0 . 7 8 \mathrm { ~ L ~ g ~ } ^ { - 1 }$ ,which was in line with the $\scriptstyle \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ values between isoproturon and the natural aquatic DOM $( 0 . 8 { - } 2 . 2 \mathrm { ~ L ~ g ~ } ^ { - 1 } )$ ）(Rizzuto et al.， 2021b). The highest and the lowest binding capacity was obtained for CBZ to PS-and PET-DOM,respectively.This result indicated that the more hydrophobic drug(CBZ) displayed a larger different binding capacity to MP-DOM than that of the relative hydrophilic drug (CAP).Besides,a decreasing trend was found for the $\scriptstyle \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ value with the initial concentration increased (i.e.,the average value of $0 . 5 \mathrm { m g L } ^ { - 1 }$ $1 \mathrm { m g L } ^ { - 1 }$ $2 \mathrm { m g L } ^ { - 1 }$ ,and $5 \mathrm { m g L } ^ { - 1 }$ was 1.06 $\mathbf { L } { \boldsymbol { g } } ^ { - 1 }$ $1 . 2 6 \mathrm { L } { \mathrm { g } } ^ { - 1 }$ $1 . 0 0 \mathrm { L } \mathrm { g } ^ { - 1 }$ $ { 0 . 6 8 \mathrm { L g } ^ { - 1 } }$ for,respectively). Similar trends were observed in the $\scriptstyle \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ value of arsenic and BPA absorbed into the soil DOM (Liu and Cai, 201O; Mo et al., 2021). In general, the logKDoc value should not be changed with the contaminant or DOM concentration,unless reaching the limit of the binding capacity (Rizzuto et al., 202la).When increasing the concentration of the contaminant, the limited binding sites of DOM definitely decrease in theory,which was verified by the decreasing $\scriptstyle \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } }$ value of arsenic and BPA with their increasing concentration.Based on this assumption,PET-CAP,PS-CAP, PET-CBZ,and PS-CBZ might reach respective limit binding capacity at the concentration of $\mathrm { C A P } = 1 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ $\mathrm { C A P } = 2 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ $\mathrm { C B Z } = 1 \ \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ ， and $\mathrm { C B Z } = 0 . 5 \mathrm { m g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ .These results were consistent with those in the above correlation analysis.

Combined with the spectral properties of ${ < } 1 \ \mathrm { k D a }$ size of MP-DOM, redundancy analysis (RDA) was used to explore the binding mechanism between MP-DOM and the pharmaceuticals (Fig. 5b). The explanation for the first and second axes was $8 8 . 8 9 \ \ \%$ and $1 0 . 9 9 \ \%$ respectively,which suggested that the binding capacity between MPDOM and CAP/CBZ was significantly impacted by spectral characteristics.As Fig.5b shows, C4 and $E _ { 2 5 3 } / E _ { 2 0 3 }$ had higher eigenvalues of PC1, while C1, C5,and $S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 }$ had higher eigenvalues of PC2. Moreover,a higher positive correlation was found between the CBZ system and DOC, $\alpha _ { 2 5 4 }$ ,C2,and C3;while the counterpart was observed between the CAP system and C4 and $E _ { 2 5 3 } / E _ { 2 0 3 }$ . It means that the humic-like substance with higher DOC and CDOM greatly influenced the binding capacity of MP-DOM vs. CBZ,and the oxygen-containing functional groups and the protein-like substance played an important role in the binding capacity of MP-DOM vs. CAP.In addition, the concentrations of the two drugs detected at different dialysis times yielded three groups along the RDA axis (Fig.5c). The initial time of CAP/CBZ concentration outside of the bag (O h) was located at the bottom-left corner of the score plot (negative scores of PCl and PC2),indicating that there was no influence by the spectral properties at the initial dialysis time.Whileall data of PET-DOM in different initial CAP/CBZ concentrations at different dialysis times had positive scores of PCl and low scores of PC2,PS-DOM had the opposite scores in contrast to PET-DOM. Combined with the result of the eigenvalues,it revealed that C4 and C1 was the key factor controlling the binding capacity of CAP/CBZ to PET-and PS-DOM,respectively. Namely, the protein-and humic-like substances have a strong effect on the binding of pharmaceuticals to PET- and PS-DOM, respectively.

# 3.4.Binding of CAP and CBZ to MP-DOM

# 3.4.1.MP-DOM binding with pharmaceuticals characterized by UV-vis and SFS

To explore the mechanism of the impact of CAP/CBZ on the conformation of MP-DOM,UV-vis absorbance,and SFS were introduced based on the fluorescence quenching titration.As the concentration of CAP/CBZ increased, the absorbance of DOM increased (Fig. S6) suggesting that a non-fluorescent complex in the mixed solution was formed as a static quenching process (Xu et al.,2Ol3).It was verified by the result of SFS.In detail,as the concentration of CAP/CBZ increased, the peaks at $2 7 8 { \mathrm { n m } }$ and $3 4 6 ~ \mathrm { { n m } }$ for PET-DOM decreased with a relatively high decreasing degree for the peak at $2 7 8 \mathrm { n m }$ (Fig. S6); while only one decreasing peak at $2 7 2 \ \mathrm { n m }$ was observed for PS-DOM.These results indicated that both the tryptophan-and the fulvic-like substances of MPDOM could be bound with CAP and CBZ,and the tryptophan-like fluorophore showed a higher binding afinity towards these drugs.

Nonetheless,the quenching degree for the same MP-DOM differed from the selected pharmaceuticals with a more pronounced variation generally presented in MP-DOM binding with CBZ.

# 3.4.2.MP-DOM binding with pharmaceuticals characterized by 2D-SFSCOS

To gain more information on the binding sequence of MP-DOM with CAP/CBZ,the synchronous and asynchronous correlation maps were drawn based on SFS (Fig.6a and b).Table S6 displayed the corresponding detailed spectral assignments.Along the diagonal line of the synchronous map (in both CAP and CBZ solutions),there were three auto-peaks located at 205,280,and $3 3 5 \mathrm { n m }$ for PET-DOM; and only one auto-peak at $2 7 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ was found for PS-DOM.Wherein,the higher fluorescence intensity was around $2 7 0 \mathrm { - } 2 8 0 ~ \mathrm { ~ n m }$ which represents the tryptophan-like substance (excluding PET-DOMvs CBZ) manifested that this fluorophore was more susceptible to the addition of CAP or CBZ. This result was in line with the binding behavior of the natural aquatic DOM to CAP/CBZ (Huang et al.,2022),demonstrating that MP-DOM had a similar binding ability to the pharmaceuticals compared with the NOM.Besides,the more auto-peaks (the tyrosine-and fulvic-like components） observed in PET-DOM revealed that more fluorophores in PET-DOM could be binding with the two drugs than those in PS-DOM. Moreover,there was a positive cross-peak at $3 3 5 / 2 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ and two negative cross-peaks at 280(335)/205 nm along the off-diagonal lines for the synchronous map in the solution of PET-DOM vs CAP,suggesting that the tryptophan-and the fulvic-like substance reduced proceeded in the same direction with increasing CAP concentration and the tyrosinelike substance decreased proceeded in the opposite direction to the two former substances with the addition of CAP.In contrast, the across-peak at $3 3 5 ( 2 0 5 ) / 2 8 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ and $3 3 5 / 2 0 5 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ in the solution of PET-DOM vs. CBZ was negative and positive,respectively. It meant that the tyrosineand the fulvic-like substance reduced proceeded in the same direction with increasing CBZ concentration and the tryptophan-like substance decreased proceeded in the opposite direction to the two former substances with the addition of CBZ. Combined with these results,it could be concluded that the same fluorescence component of MP-DOM showeddifferentbindingbehaviortowardsthedifferent pharmaceuticals.

# 3.4.3.MP-DOM binding with pharmaceuticals characterized by 2D-FTIRCOS

The binding sequence of functional groups in MP-DOM with the two drugs was proceeded by 2D-FTIR-COS analyzing the region of 1800-700 $\mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 }$ (Chen et al., 2O15), where the main information about binding sites appeared.In the synchronous map of the four binding systems exhibited several auto-peaks at 800,1080(1020),1260,1350,1520 (1490),1690 (1600),and $1 7 0 0 \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 } ( \mathrm { F i g . } 6 $ c and d),which were assigned to the C-H in aromatics,polysaccharides C—O,aliphatic C-OH,phenolic $\mathrm { O - H } ,$ amide $\mathrm { I I } \ \mathrm { N \mathrm { - } H } ,$ amide ${ \mathrm { I } } { \mathrm { C } } { = } 0$ ,and carboxyl $\scriptstyle { \mathsf { C } } = 0$ (Lee and Hur, 2020; Tang et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2021). It indicated that these functional groups were involved in the binding of MP-DOM with the pharmaceuticals.Furthermore,the binding sites were more similar for the same drug than those for the MP-DOM types.For instance,the phenolic $\mathrm { O - H }$ $( 1 3 5 0 ~ \mathrm { c m } ^ { - 1 } )$ and the amide $\mathrm { I } { \mathrm { ~ C } } { = } 0$ (1600 and $1 6 9 0 ~ \mathrm { { c m } ^ { - 1 } ) }$ were only found for the CAP and CBZ binding systems,respectively.This result was in line with those in 2D-SFS-COS that the binding behavior was mainly dependent on the pharmaceuticals rather than the MP-DOM.

Interestingly,the binding sequence also showed more similarity for the same drug than those for the MP-DOM types.According to Noda's principle, the binding sequence of the functional groups in CAP binding systems was ordered as polysaccharides $\mathbf { C } { \cdot } \mathbf { O } >$ aliphatic $\mathrm { C } { \cdot } \mathrm { O H } >$ amide I $\mathrm { C = O _ { P E T - D O M } }$ /carboxyl $\mathrm { C = O _ { P S - D O M } > }$ phenolic $\mathrm { O } { \cdot } \mathrm { H } >$ amide II $\mathrm { N \mathrm { - } H > C \mathrm { - } }$ H in aromatics;while the sequence of functional groups on MP-DOM participating in binding with CBZ was observed as polysaccharides C$\mathrm { o } >$ aliphatic $\mathrm { C } { \cdot } \mathrm { O H } >$ (amide II $\mathrm { N - H _ { P S - D O M } ) > }$ amide I ${ \mathrm { C } } { = } 0 >$ (carboxyl $\mathbf { C } { = } \mathbf { O } _ { \mathbf { P E T - D O M } } ) > \mathbf { C } { \mathbf { - } } \mathbf { H }$ in aromatics (Fig. 6 c, d and Fig. 7). These binding behavior of the functional groups between MP-DOM and the pharmaceuticals demonstrated that:1) there was more binding sites of MP-DOM when reacted with CAP compared to CBZ; 2) irrespective of the types of the functional groups,the oxygen-containing functional groups (C一O, $\mathrm { O - H } ,$ and ${ \mathrm { C } } { = } 0$ possessed higher attractions with the pharmaceuticals, which agreed with the NOMor MP-DOM binding with the heavy metals (Fan et al.， 2023; Lee et al.， 202la). Furthermore， these oxygencontaining functional groups had been reported to the fundamental fluorescent compositions on the humic-and protein-like substances (Fan et al.,2022).Combining the results of the 2D-SFS-COS,it could be known that the fluorophores having more oxygen-containing binding sitesforMP-DOMcouldpreferentiallyinteractwiththe pharmaceuticals.

# 3.4.4.Changes in the fluorescence components in MP-DOM binding with pharmaceuticals

In order to quantify the binding affinity of MP-DOMwith the pharmaceuticals,the changes in fluorescence components of MP-DOM binding with pharmaceuticals were investigated (Fig. S7).It showed that Cl and C4 could be quenched by the addition of the two drugs to a varying degree.Whereas no significant declines were observed for the other three components.In general,when the concentration of pharmaceuticals reached $1 0 \mathrm { m g L } ^ { - 1 }$ ,about $5 8 \%$ of the fluorescence intensity was eliminated after adding CAP or CBZ for C1 and C4 components.

Furthermore,the fluorescence intensity at all the concentrations of CAP/CBZ in the four solutions was plotted to visually display the different binding affinity between C1 and C4,CAP and CBZ,and PET and PS (Fig. S8).It could be seen that the fluorescence intensity of CAP vs. CBZ was close to the diagonal line (the red dots),while the counterparts of C1 vs.C4 and PETvs.PS were close to the X-axis (the blue dots) and Yaxis (the green dots),respectively.These results illustrated that:1) the binding affinity between the protein-like substance (C4)and the selected drugs was higher than that for the marine humic-like substance (C1); 2) PET-DOM exhibited a higher binding affinity towards the pharmaceuticals;3) a slightly higher binding affinity was shown between MP-DOM vs CBZ.

# 3.5.Environmental implication

With the pollution of MPs becoming more and more serious,the influence of MP-DOM on the fate of the other contaminants was inevitable and gradually gained more attention.This study is the first to investigate the binding mechanism between MP-DOM and pharmaceuticals based on spectroscopic features.By comparing the binding capacity $( \log K _ { \mathrm { D O C } } )$ and affinity $( \mathrm { l o g } K _ { \mathrm { M } } )$ of NOM binding with pollutants in previous studies,MP-DOM was demonstrated to exhibit comparable values to the NOM.It indicated that MP-DOMcould impact the fate and transport of not only the metals (Lee et al., 2O2la) but also the organic pollutants (e.g.，pharmaceuticals).However,this binding effect was often neglected in some studies that the overlooked MP-DOM could compete with the NOM to bind with the pollutants in the realistic environment,especially in heavily MP-polluted environments.Therefore,distinguishing and identifying the impact of MP-DOM and the NOM in natural environments when coexisting is needed in the future, because of their similar environmental behavior towards the pollutants.

# 4. Conclusions

This study firstly and systematically promoted of the understanding the characteristics of MP-DOM with different sizes and their effects on the binding behavior of the pharmaceuticals (i.e.,CAP and CBZ) through multiple spectral technologies and methods.Afteraging by UV irradiation,the DOC concentration in MP-DOM released from PET and PS increased.Humic-like substances were released preferentially and more from MPs,with tryptophan-like fluorophores more sensitive in this process.Regardless of the MP types,MP-DOM had a stronger binding capacity for CAP than CBZ.Despite the more sensitive fluorescence separation of protein-like substances in MP-DOM during dialysis,the transferred sequence of the fluorophores depended on the types of MPDOM. Moreover, the fluorescence substances with oxygen-containing functional groups such as polysaccharides C—O and aliphatic C-OH preferred binding to the pharmaceuticals.Results of the fluorescence quenching titration indicated that the susceptibility and sequence of the MP-DOM vs.pharmaceuticals binding system significantly depend on the pharmaceuticals and the higher binding affinity was related to the protein-like substance,CBZ,and PET-DOM.
