Increased algal blooms and lossof aquatic vegetationare critical environmental isses associated with shallow lakes worldwide.The increase in organic mater (OM) in both macrophyte-dominated areas (MDAs)and algaedominated areas (ADAs) has exacerbated these problems.Most OM in water is concentrated as suspended particulate mater (SPM),which eventually migrates to the sediment.However,the detailed originsand fates of OM in water-SPM-sediment systems with coexisting MDAs and ADAs remain unclear.Therefore,in this study,we conducted monthly field investigations in Lake Taihu,focusing on OM-migration pattrns inan MDA and an ADA. The C/N mass ratios, $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ contents,and OM compositions of the water,SPM,and sediment were analyzed. Our findings revealed that autochthonous sources of OM prevailed in water,whereas terrestrial sources prevailed in SPM and sediment.Rapid decomposition processes of microbial- and algae-derived dissolved OM were discovered along the water-SPM-sediment pathways in both areas.A trend towards a shift from macrophytes to algae in the MDA was also discovered.Overall,the entire lake underwent a burial processof OM in both types of areas,with mineralization mostly occurring during the algal-bloom seasons and more strongly in the ADA. Furthermore,we deduced thata decrease in the OM-burial rate,butan increase in the mineralization rate, might occur after a complete shift from a macrophyte-to an algae-dominated status.Such a shift might change the carbon-cycle process in eutrophic shallow lakes and should be given more attention in future research.

# 1．Introduction

Eutrophication increases harmful algal blooms and decreases aquatic vegetation in lakes.Globally,approximately $6 8 \%$ of lakes have experienced increased algal bloom levels (Ho et al.,2019),especially in the past decade (Hou et al., 2O22).During the same period, the aquatic vegetation area of approximately $6 5 \%$ of all lakes decreased (Zhang et al.,2Ol7). Excessive nutrient inputs,including carbon (C),nitrogen (N),and phosphorus (P),are believed to trigger or aggravate these processes (Andersen et al.,202O; Liang et al., 202O). Increased algal blooms and decreased vegetation result in the massive accumulation and decomposition of algae and plant debris,which further increases the concentrations of C,N,and P in lakes (Cheng et al., 2O23).Nand P are usually considered limiting elements in algal blooms (Schindler et al., 2016; Wu et al., 2022), whereas C levels (mostly inorganic) are usually sufficiently high in water to promote the growth of algae.Recent findings have revealed that with the development of eutrophication,the cumulative levels of organic matter (OM) in water and sediments have tended to increase in many lakes (Fujibayashi et al., 2O19; Meyer-Jacob et al.,2Ol9). Consequently, the amount of research focusing on the origins and fates of OM is increasing annually (Derrien et al., 2019).

The degradation of increased OM in lakes will lead to oxygen depletion and increased N and P levels (Derrien et al., 2O19; Joshi et al., 2015). Oxygen depletion will accelerate the release of N and P from the sediment (Hupfer and Lewandowski, 2008; Sima et al., 202O). Moreover,increased OM also increases the concentrations of organic N and P in the water and sediment (Ni et al.,2022; Wymore et al.,2021). Increased organic N and P levels have been considered crucial for subsequent increases in Nand P levels in water bodies,which has attractd mounting attention as eutrophication has increased in the past two decades (Gao et al., 2022; Ni et al., 2022; Valdemarsen et al., 2015). In addition,high concentrations of OM in water can inhibit photosynthesis in submerged plants,thereby decreasing the aquatic vegetation areas and abundances in many freshwater bodies (Reitsema et al., 2018).

Consequently,increased OM exacerbates the eutrophication status in lakes by accelerating the accumulation of nutrients and algae and inhibiting aquatic plant growth. Therefore,the origin, transformation, and migration of OMin lake ecosystems represent crucial factors in the global development of lake eutrophication.

OM in lakes is comprised of various compounds,including humic matter, lignin, cellulose,and amino acids (Derrien et al.,2O19).Due to the input of numerous compounds and pollutants from different sources, rivers,and surface runoff of the entire watershed, the origin and fate of OM in lakes are complex.Generally,the OM can be classified as allochthonous or autochthonous (Derrien et al., 2O19).Many natural sources of allochthonous OM exist,including terrestrial plant debris, soil erosion, and atmospheric deposition (Jaffé et al., 2Ol3; Monteith et al., 2007;Shi et al., 2O2O).However, drastic anthropogenic activities can lead to a massive input of OM, for example, through industrial manufacture,sewage discharge,agricultural production,and other mechanisms (Carstea et al., 2016; Li et al., 2016; Wymore et al., 2021). The primary sources of autochthonous origin include phytoplankton,fish, benthic organisms, and macrophytes (Liu et al., 2O19c; Reitsema et al., 2018； Zhang et al.，2Oo9).In eutrophic lakes, the degradation of blooming algae and macrophyte debris is a major source of OM(Ouyang et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2009).

OM in lakes is usually divided into dissolved (DOM) and particulate pools and is transformed quickly between pools (Orellana and Leck, 2015).In eutrophic shallow lakes,abundant suspended particulate matter (SPM) usually adsorbs high levels of OM because of their fine particle sizes (Liu et al., 2Ol9a).Therefore,concentrated SPM represents a crucial medium for OM transformation and migration between water, SPM,and sediment.Furthermore,eutrophic shallow lakes usually face a shift from a macrophyte-dominated to an algae-dominated status (Schefer et al., 200l; Scheffer and Van Nes, 2007). Brothers et al. (2013) observed increased C burial after a shift from macrophytes to algae occurred.Wang et al. (2Ol6) revealed more mobile pools of C after this type of shift.With the co-existence of macrophyte-dominated area (MDA)and algae-dominated area (ADA),distinct SPM origins and

SPM-deposition or -resuspension processes render the origins and fates of OM in lakes unclear.Therefore,we hypothesize that the abundantly accumulated SPM in eutrophic shallow lakes might change the origins and fates of OM along the water-SPM-sediment pathway in the MDA and ADA,which might further lead to changes in the burial and mineralization processes of OMwith the shift from a macrophyte- to an algae-dominated status.In this study,we conducted monthly field observations of OMin a water-SPM-sediment system of an MDA and ADA in Lake Taihu. We sought to reveal the origin and fate of OM in the large eutrophic shallow lake with a coexisting MDA and ADA and to uncover the burial and mineralization processes of OM in both areas.

# 2．Materials and methods

# 2.1． Study site and sample collection

Lake Taihu is a large,shallow,and eutrophic lake located in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River.During the past few decades,the lake has undergone a continuous increase in algal blooms (Guo et al., 2022) and a loss of aquatic vegetation (Dong et al., 2022), leading to an increase in the ADA and a decrease in the MDA.The current macrophyte coverage is approximately $1 0 . 6 \%$ of the entire lake area (Liu et al., 2O22). # 2.2. Stable-isotope $( \delta ^ { 1 3 } C )$ and $C / N$ mass ratio analysis of the SPM and sediment samples

All SPM and sediment samples were freeze-dried under a vacuum and ground to powder (sieved through $0 . 1 5 0 \ \mathrm { m m }$ mesh size) before further analysis.To analyze the $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ in the SPM and sediment, the ground samples were first treated with $3 ~ \mathrm { m o l } ~ L ^ { - 1 }$ HCl to remove carbonates and then rinsed five times with ultrapure water.The rinsed samples were freeze-dried and ground into a powder.The pretreated samples were then analyzed in an elemental analyzer (EA 3ooo,EuroVector,Italy） connected to a stable-isotope ratio mass spectrometer (Finnigan MAT Delta V,Thermo Finnigan,America) to analyze $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ levels.Isotopic ratios were reported in standard delta (δ)units relative to the Vienna PeeDee Belemnite standard. Standard samples were run once every five samples. The precision of the $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ measurement was $\pm$ $0 . 1 0 \%$ .The total organic carbon(TOC) levels,total nitrogen (TN) levels, and C/N mass ratios (TOC/TN) of the SPM and sediment samples were directly analyzed using an elemental analyzer.Standard sediment samples and duplicate samples were analyzed once every 1O samples, with a relative error and relative standard deviation of less than $5 \%$ and $1 \%$ ,respectively.

# 2.3.Analysis of DOC and DOM levels in the water,SPM,and sediment samples

The water samples were filtered through $0 . 4 5 \mu \mathrm { m }$ membranes before analyzing DOC and DOM levels.DOC and DOM in the SPM and sediment were extracted in ultrapure water for $2 4 \mathrm { h }$ at a solid/water ratio of1/20 $\mathrm { ( m / v ) }$ .The extracts were then centrifuged and filtered through $0 . 4 5 \mu \mathrm { m }$ membranes before analysis.The DOC in the extracts and filtered water samples were analyzed usinga TOC analyzer (Torch,Teledyne Tekmar, Mason, OH, USA).The excitation-emission matrix fluorescence spectra of the extracts and filtered water samples were analyzed in a Fluorolog?-3 spectrometer (HORIBA Instruments,Incorporated,Edison, NJ,USA).The excitation and emission scanning ranges were 200-450 nm ( $\mathtt { 7 } \mathtt { n m }$ intervals) and $2 4 0 { - } 1 0 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ (1 nm intervals),respectively. Ultrapure water was scanned to obtain a blank excitation-emission matrix and eliminate the Raman-scattering peaks for water.The spectral characteristics of the extracts and filtered water samples were obtained using a spectrophotometer (UV 2550, Shimadzu,Kyoto, Japan) with a $2 0 0 { - } 8 0 0 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ scanning range (1 nm intervals).The excitation-emission matrix spectra were then decomposed via parallel factor analysis using the DOMFluor toolbox (http://www.models.life.ku.dk/) (Murphy et al., 2013).The parallel factor procedure was described in detail previously (Ohno et al., 2007; Stedmon and Bro, 2008). The models obtained were matched using the OpenFluor database (https://openfluor.lablicate. com/) (Murphy et al., 2O14). The humification index, biological index, fluorescence index,absorption-spectrum slope,and slope ratio were calculated as described previously (Helms et al., 2Oo8; Huguet et al., 2009; McKnight et al., 2001; Zsolnay et al., 1999).

# 2.4.Analysis of other physicochemical properties

The structures of SPM samples were analyzed using a scanning electron microscope (EVO 18,Zeiss,Germany). The freeze-dried SPM samples were imaged at $2 0 0 ~ \times$ and $2 0 0 0 \ \times$ magnification using a scanning electron microscope with an accelerating voltage of $2 0 \mathrm { k V } .$ The total suspended solids (TSSs) in the water were analyzed by filtering SPM samples through $0 . 4 5 \mu \mathrm { m }$ glass fiber membranes and drying them to a constant weight.Chlorophyll a in the water was extracted with $9 0 \%$ acetone and analyzed using an ultraviolet and visible spectrophotometer (UV2550, Shimadzu, Japan) (Chinese-EPA, 2002).

# 2.5.Statistical analysis

MATLAB software (version R2019b;Mathworks,USA) was used to analyze the excitation-emission matrix spectra and plot the component pictures.Paired t-tests and one-way analysis of variance (ANOVA) were performed to analyze differences in various characteristics.Pearson's or Spearman's correlations were analyzed to identify the relationships between different parameters.Significance is reported at the $p < 0 . 0 5$ and $p < 0 . 0 1$ levels. Statistical analyses were performed using SPSS (version 26.O; IBM,Armonk,NY,USA).Data were graphed using Origin software (version 202l; OriginLab,USA).

# 4．Discussion

# 4.1.Origins of the deposited SPM and associated OM in the MDA and ADA

Significant differences were usually observed in the environmental characteristics of MDA and ADA (two typical ecotypes in shallow lakes). Generally, the MDA exhibited a clear state when it contained a low TSS concentration in the water and a low amount of resuspended sediment, whereas a turbid state corresponded with high phytoplankton biomass and TSS in the water and a high amount of resuspended sediment (Brothers et al.,2013; Scheffer and Van Nes, 2007). As described in Section 3.1 above,the annual sedimentation rate of SPMin the ADA was approximately seven times higher than that in the MDA,accompanied by higher TSS and chlorophyll a concentrations (Fig.2).The high sedimentation rate of the ADA in summer was consistent with the high algal biomass and blooming area (Guo et al.，2O22).The distinct environmental characteristics of both areas resulted in different SPM origins.In both areas,the C/N ratios of the SPMand sediment were less than 12,with the C/N ratio in the SPM mostly lower than 8 and the C/N ratio in the sediment mostly between 8 and 1o.In addition, the $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ values for both the SPMand the sediment ranged from $- 2 8 \%$ to $- 2 2 \%$ (Fig.4). These results indicate that a mix of internal (macrophytes and phytoplankton) and external sources influenced the characteristics of both the MDA and ADA according to previous findings (Cifuentes et al., 1996; Lamb et al., 2006; Ogrinc et al., 2008). However, the prevailing sources in both areas difered. The $\delta ^ { 1 3 } \mathrm { C }$ values in SPM and sediment were higher in the MDA than in the ADA (Fig.S4),indicating a greater presence of phytoplankton in the ADA (Lamb et al., 2O06). The higher TN concentrations in MDA SPM and sediment samples (Section 3.3) and the larger particle sizes (Section 3.6) were also indicative of a stronger influence of macrophyte decay on the OM than in the ADA,as observed in other studies (Li et al., 2014; Wang et al., 2018). In addition, the range of C/N values was broader in the ADA than in the MDA (Fig. S4), demonstrating a more complex origin of OM in the ADA.

# 4.2.Differentiated fates of the OM in the water-SPM-sediment systems of the MDA and ADA

Most OM in eutrophic lake water is concentrated in SPM (Derrien et al.,2O19).Therefore,the fate of OMin SPM largely determines the behavior of OM in the entire water body.As key internal sources of SPM in water,algae and macrophyte debris undergo distinct degradation processes after deposition.Previous data showed that rapid algae degradation usually occurs after approximately 2-7 days of accumulation and deposition (Liu et al., 2Ol5). The protein content of algae can be as high as $5 5 \mathrm { - } 7 0 \%$ (Gatenby et al., 2003). Similarly in Lake Taihu, Li (2009) also discovered that the protein content of algae was higher than $4 0 \%$ .High proportions of proteins lead to rapid decomposition of algal debris,whereas macrophyte degradation takes much longer (Chimney and Pietro, 2006; Li et al., 2021). The distinct degradation processes of debris from algae and macrophytes gave rise to differentiated OM distributions and patterns in the water-SPM-sediment systems of MDA and ADA.

As described in Section 4.1,both areas had mixed sources for the OM in the water, SPM,and sediment.The high biomass of algae in the ADA (Section 3.1) was consistent with the high proportions of algae-derived DOM.Increased algae concentrations in the MDA have also been discovered in recent years (Li et al.,2O23).Algae-derived SPM was quickly deposited on the surface sediment,accompanied by quick decomposition.The results of a previous study conducted in Lake Taihu showed that the decomposition of the deposited algal particles led to an almost complete depletion of oxygen within 2 days (Liu et al., 2015), and substantial hypoxia has been frequently discovered in the ADA of Lake Taihu in recent years (Fan, 2015; Liu etal., 2O19b). The slope ratio values of DOMin the water,SPM,and sediment samples generally followed a descending order (Fig.S5 and Fig.S6).The proportions of Comp 4(autochthonous protein-like materials) sharply decrease following the same order (water, SPM,and sediment; Section 3.5),accompanied by decreased microbial-derived and algae-derived DOM levels (Section 4.1).These trends indicate that microbial-derived and algae-derived DOM rapidly decomposed along the water-SPM-sediment pathway for both areas.Importantly,both the MDA and ADA exhibited similar OM-decomposition patterns along the water-SPM-sediment path.This trend is consistent with the mixed sources for OM described in Section 4.1.That is, the MDA in Lake Taihu is now shifting from a macrophyteto an algae-dominated status,which merits further attention.

Eutrophic lakes usually have high TSS concentrations because of their high aquatic biomass (Behnke et al.， 2023). In shallow lakes, resuspended sediment further increases the TSS concentrations in water (Qi et al., 2O19).The distribution of macrophytes in shallow lakes limits sediment resuspension (Horppila and Nurminen, 2Oo3). In this study, the TOCs in the SPMand sediment showed a negative correlation in the MDA but a positive correlation in the ADA (Fig.7),indicating that resuspended sediment influenced the SPM more strongly in the ADA.In contrast,the DOC levels in the SPM and sediment were positively correlated in the MDA but negatively correlated in the ADA.The DOC/TOC ratio was higher in the ADA than in the MDA (Section 3.2), suggesting that the SPMin the ADA was influenced both by resuspended sediments and a high algae biomass (Section 3.1).The high DOC/TOC ratio indicates higher OMdegradation potential in the ADA than in the MDA (Begum et al., 2023). In the ADA, a positive correlation $( p < 0 . 0 1$ Figs.S7and Fig.S8) between the TOC in the SPM and the DOM in water and SPMfurther indicated the higher degradation potential of OM in the SPM.

Overall, our results indicate that the OM-burial processes dominated in both the MDA and ADA,with those in the MDA being stronger than those in the ADA.Previous data revealed that OM burial increased with a shift from macrophytes to phytoplankton (Brothers et al.,2Ol3) and that the C pool in the sediment also increased during such shifts (Wang et al., 2016).However,our current findings further revealed that the C-burial rate maydecreasegreatly aftera completeshiftfroma macrophyte-dominated status to an algae-dominated status.Moreover, the high OM-degradation potential and greater differences in the DOC in the ADAindicated that mineralization increased underan algae-dominated status,particularly during the algal-bloom season. The overall processes of OMburial and mineralization in the MDA and ADA are depicted in Fig.8.The ADA generally showed a lower OM-burial rate but a higher mineralization rate.Such different fates for OM in both areas should be followed during eutrophication and a shift from a macrophyte-to an algae-dominated status in shallow lakes,which could decrease C burial and increase C release on a global scale.

# 5．Conclusions

Monthly field investigations in the MDA and ADA of Lake Taihu revealeda higher sedimentation rate for SPMin the ADAbut higher TOC concentrations in the MDA.OM in water,SPM,and sediment samples obtained from both areas originated from mixed autochthonous and terrestrial sources,with autochthonous sources prevailing in the ADA. Similar,quick decomposition patterns of microbial-derived and algaederived DOM were revealed in both the MDA and ADA along the water-SPM-sediment pathway.It is noteworthy that a potential deterioration of algal blooms was discovered in the MDA,indicating more attention should be given to this area during future research and management of eutrophication in Lake Taihu.Moreover,overall burial processes were discovered in both areas,with the mineralization process mostly occurring during the bloom seasons and at higher rates in the ADA.The decreased OM-burial rate but increased mineralization rate in the ADA might alter the C-cycle process with a shift froma macrophyteto algae-dominated status in shallow lakes,which should be monitored closely in future research.
