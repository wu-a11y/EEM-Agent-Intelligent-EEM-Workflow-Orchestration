Aquaculture is a major type of human activity that disturbs the biogeochemistry and ecosystem in coastal environments,yet its influence on the optical properties of particulate organic matter remains largely unknown. This was studied for the Aojiang Estuary-cultural zone-adjacent seawater continuum in SE China in July and October 2019,and in January 2020,using absorption spectroscopy and fluorescence excitation-emission matrices-parallel factor analysis (EEMs-ARAFAC).The POM absorption,three humic-like components (C1, C2,and C5),and humification index (HIX) generall decreased from the river to the seawaterand were higher in October than in January.The humic substances were mainly derived from terrestrial sources and sediment resuspension,which were dependent largely on the level of total suspended mater $( r ; 0 . 8 2 3 - 0 . 8 8 8 )$ . The intensity of shoulder peak in the absorption spectra and the tryptophan-like C4 were lower in the river than in seawater in July,which was in contrast to the spatial distribution inother months.They correlated positively with Chla (r:0.779-0.912),indicating they were mainly related to thealgal production.Principal component analysis (PCA)revealed notable seasonal variations of POMwith an elevated autochthonous contribution in July, indicating stronger algal production than the removal by shelfish.The low autochthonous POM in October and January identified with PCA suggested limited addition from the laver culture after the shelfish harvest.The mooring monitoring in Julyand October furtherdemonstrated that the POM in the cultural zoneand adjacent seawater was influenced by the active material exchange acros the continuum,strong tides,sediment resuspension,algal production,and microbial transformation. Our results have implications for assessing the influences of aquaculture activities on the water quality and ecosystem in the coastal environments.

# 1．Introduction

Particulate organic matter (POM) plays an important role in the global carbon cycle,water quality,and aquatic ecosystem function. The level of POM (measured as particulate organic carbon, POC) generally decreased from global rivers (mean: $3 7 7 . 5 \ \mu \mathrm { M }$ ,Ludwig et al., 1996) to the ocean (mean: $1 6 . 5 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } _ { : }$ ，Martiny et al., 2014),although it changes greatly in different systems.Annually, ${ \sim } 0 . 2 \ \mathrm { P g }$ of POC is discharged from land to the ocean,which is mixed with the POC from aquatic production (Ludwig et al., 1996; Blair and Aller, 2012). The transport, degradation, sinking,and burial of terrestrial and autochthonous POM in the estuarine and coastal environments is a key component in the global carbon budget (Hedges et al., 1997; Bianchi et al., 2011; Bauer et al.,20l3). POM is also important for determining the water quality and ecosystem functioning (e.g.，consuming oxygen during POM degradation,complexing with pollutants,and serving as a material and energy source for aquatic organisms,Hu et al.，2O19; Wang et al., 2020a).The source,chemical composition，and fate of POM are increasingly affected by the fast-growing aquaculture activities in coastal environments (Moffitt and Cajas-Cano, 2O14; Sara et al., 2004; Zhang et al., 2O17; Jean-Marc et al., 2018). In particular, the culture of shellfish is a major type of coastal aquaculture,which accounts for $6 0 \%$ of the seawater aquaculture area and $7 0 \%$ of the seawater aquaculture yield in 2019 in China (Yu et al.,202O). Therefore, it is important to test the influences of shellfish culture on the dynamics of POM in coastal environments.

The effects of aquaculture activities on POM are largely dependent on the cultural species.The cultured algae can produce abundant organic matter such as proteins and carbohydrates by photosynthesis, which can change the elemental ratio of POM in the seawater (Wada et al., 2007; Cade-Menun et al., 201O; Buchholz etal., 2016). The fish culture can introduce additional POM as uneaten fish feed, fish fecal, and enhanced algal production due to elevated nutrient levels (Sara et al., 2004; Rubio-Portillo et al., 2019; Chaves et al., 2020; Wang et al., 2020a). Once scattered onto the field, the shellfish feeds on the coastal aquatic plankton or organic detritus,and no additional feeds are manually added.This way of aquaculture eventually removes POM from the seawater and in part reduce the eutrophication (Filgueira et al., 2015; Sanz-Lazaro etal.,2Ol8). In addition, the formation of soft tissues and shells can remove carbon from the seawater (Zhang et al., 2017), and the uptake of dissolved organic carbon by shellfish can exceed its release (Mahmood et al., 2017).

While previous studies identified the influences of shellfish on the POMcycle in the coastal seawater, these results are mainly based on the measurement of bulk carbon parameters and the influences on the chemical composition of POM are largely unknown. Absorption spectroscopy and fluorescence excitation-emission matrices-parallel factor analysis (EEMs-PARAFAC)are popular tools for characterizing dissolved organic matter in aquatic environments and engineering systems (Helms et al., 2008; Stedmon and Bro, 2008; Yang et al., 2017; Liu et al., 2019; Deng et al., 202O; Luo and Li, 2021). These techniques have also been successfully used for tracking the changes of chromophoric and fluorescent POM(CPOM and FPOM) (Osburn et al., 2012,2015, 2019; Yang et al., 2013; Brym et al., 2014; Wang et al., 2020a, b; Qu et al., 2021). However,few studies applied these techniques for studying the chemical composition of POM and for tracing the spatial/temporal changes and underlying mechanisms of individual fluorescent components in the coastal cultural areas.

Therefore,this study aims to:(1) characterize the spatial and temporal changes in the chemical composition and individual fluorescent components of POM in a typical estuary-cultural zone-adjacent seawater continuum of SE China,using absorption spectroscopy and fluorescence EEMs-PARAFAC;and (2) unveil the underlying mechanisms for the changes of POM and assess the impacts of coastal culture,based on correlation analysis,multiple linear regression,principal component analysis,and diurnal monitoring at mooring stations.The results would help to assess the effects of aquaculture activities on the water quality and ecosystem in the coastal ocean.

# 2.Materials and methods

# 2.1. Study area and field sampling

The Lianjiang County (Fuzhou City,SE China) is the 2nd largest county of China in fishery products over the last three decades.The Aojiang Estuary Cultural Zone is one of the six main cultural zones of the Lianjiang County,which consists of more than 11oo ha of beaches for shellfish culture (Fig.1). The main cultural species are Ruditapes philippinarum in sandy beaches and Sinonovacula Constricta (Lamarck) in clay beaches,which are seeded around March and are harvested in September.The sediment is manually turned over during the shellfish harvest and thereafter laver is cultured until January of the next year. The cultural zone is located outside the Aojiang Estuary and is connected with the East China Sea.The Aojiang River is a medium-sized river with a length of $1 3 7 \ \mathrm { k m }$ ，a watershed area of $2 6 5 5 ~ \mathrm { k m } ^ { 2 }$ ，and an annual freshwater discharge of about 2.728 billion $\mathfrak { m } ^ { 3 }$ (Zhang et al., 2014). The local annual precipitation is $1 0 5 6 \mathrm { - } 1 6 8 0 \mathrm { m m }$ ,about half of which occurs in the rainy season from May to August (http://www.cma.gov.cn). In the sampling years,the monthly precipitation was high during June-July 2019,and in March and May-June 202O (Fig.S1).

Water samples were collected on July 4th-1Oth and October 15th2lst,2019,and in January 9th-11th,2020,which corresponded to the periods of shellfish culture,shellfish harvest plus laver culture,and laver harvest,respectively.The sampling stations covered the Aojiang Estuary,cultural zone,and the adjacent coastal sea (Fig.1).To examine the diurnal variations and exchanges between the cultural and coastal sea zones,two mooring stations were sampled every $^ { 6 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ over $2 4 \ \mathrm { h }$ during July 7th-8th (at Y1 and W2)and October 20th-2lst (at Y1 and W4), 2019.The sampling time of $2 4 \mathrm { h }$ covered two tidal cycles,since the study area is dominated by semi-diurnal tides.At each sampling time,about $^ { 1 0 \mathrm { ~ L ~ } }$ water sample was pooled together for further filtration and measurements.A total of 145 samples were analyzed, including 121 from extensive observations and 24 from mooring observations.

The slope over 275-295 nm $( S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 } )$ was calculated from the absorption spectra,which correlated negatively with the aromaticity and average molecular weight of organic matter (Helms et al., 2008). $\Delta a _ { 2 8 0 }$ was calculated for assessing the intensity of the shoulder peak around 280 nm,which was the difference between the measured $a _ { 2 8 0 }$ and the fitted value extrapolated from the linear regression of log-transformed absorbance over $3 0 0 { \ - } 4 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ (Tilstone et al., 2010; Brym et al., 2014; Wang et al., 2020a).

Fluorescence EEMs were scanned over excitation (Ex) 240-450 and emission (Em $) 2 8 0 \mathrm { - } 6 0 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ,using a Cary Eclipse fluorescence spectrophotometer (Yang et al., 2013, 2019). The measured EEMs were corrected for inner-filter effects based on absorption spectra (McKnight etal.,2OO1) and subtracted the blank signals of Milli-Q water.Each EEM was corrected for the volumes of NaOH,HCl,and filtered water (Osburn et al.,2012).A total of 145 EEMs were modeled with PARAFAC in MATLAB 7.0 using the DOMFluor toolbox 1.7 (Stedmon and Bro, 2008; Yang et al.,2O17). The number of PARAFAC components was determined based on core consistency test and split-half validation,and the fluorescence intensity $( F _ { \mathrm { m a x } } )$ of each component was used to represent itslevel in Raman Unit (RU; Stedmon and Bro, 2Oo8).Humification index (HIX)and biological index (BIX) were also calculated from EEMs, which were indices for the humic content and source of organic matter respectively (Zsolnay et al., 1999; Huguet et al., 2009). HIX was the ratio of fluorescence over $\mathrm { E m } \ 4 3 4 { - } 4 8 0 \ \mathrm { n m }$ to that over $3 0 0 { - } 3 4 6 ~ \mathrm { n m }$ (at Ex $2 5 5 \mathrm { n m } )$ ,andBIX was the ratio of fluorescence at $\mathrm { E m } 3 8 0 \mathrm { n m }$ to that at $4 3 0 \mathrm { n m }$ (at $\operatorname { E x } 3 1 0 \mathrm { n m }$ ).The triplicate analytical precision was $2 . 5 7 \% \pm$ $0 . 0 2 \%$ for absorption indices and $4 . 2 1 \% \pm 0 . 0 3 \%$ for fluorescence indices.

Correlation analysis,stepwise multiple linear regression,principal component analysis (PCA),and $t$ -test for comparing different groups of samples were carried out in SPSs Statistics 22. Correlation analysis was tested with the Pearson correlation coefficient $( r )$ and the significance level $( p )$ . In the stepwise multiple linear regression,each independent variable was included if it could improve the predictionat $p \leq 0 . 0 5$ .The final multiple linear regression was assessed with the Pearson correlation coefficient $( r )$ and the significance level $( p )$ for the correlation between the measured and the predicted values.

# 4.Discussion

# 4.1.Spatial and seasonal changes of POM in the estuary-cultural zoneadjacent seawater continuum: effects of environmental factors and coastal culture

The optical indices for the quantity and quality of POM showed notable spatial and seasonal variations in this study (Figs.2-3; Table 1 and S1).These results,together with correlation analysis,multiple linear regression,and PCA,are examined to unveil the effects of environmental factors and coastal culture on POM. First, $a _ { 3 0 0 }$ and humic-like C1, C2, and C5 correlated strongly with each other,suggesting similar behaviors of bulk CPOM and humic-like fluorescent components $( r ; 0 . 9 8 6 - 0 . 9 9 6$ $p < 0 . 0 1$ ,Table 2).They generally decreased from the river end to the ocean and were lowest in January for all three zones likely due to the decreasing rainfall and fluvial discharge in the dry season (Fig.S1).The negative correlations between these indices and salinity indicated significant influences of mixing between freshwater and seawater (r: $- 0 . 7 4 1$ to $- 0 . 7 7 9$ $p < 0 . 0 1$ , Table 2; Zhao and Gao., 2019). In addition, ä30o and humic-like components were largely dependent on the changes of TSM $( r \colon 0 . 8 2 3  – 0 . 8 8 8$ $p < 0 . 0 1 $ ),which could be affected by resuspension and coastal erosion besides the fluvial sediment discharge (Semiletov et al., 201l; Wang et al., 2020b). For example, the higher $a _ { 3 0 0 }$ and humic-like components in October were probably related to the elevated TSM, which was most evident in the estuarine zone (Fig. S4). The positive correlations of $a _ { 3 0 0 }$ and humic-like components with Chl a $( r \colon 0 . 4 8 3  – 0 . 5 7 7$ ， $p < 0 . 0 1 \AA$ ),further indicated the potential moderate contribution of autochthonous production (Mazzola et al., 2Ool). Previous studies have shown the increase of color organic matter,and the protein-like and humic-like fluorophores by algal growth and decay (Romera-Castillo et al., 201O; Hong et al., 2012). The stepwise multiple linear regression confirmed that the changing salinity,TSM,and Chl a were important for explaining the variations of bulk CPOM and humic-like components $( r ; 0 . 9 6 0  – 0 . 9 8 6$ $\mathbf { p } < 0 . 0 1$ ,Fig. 6). In particular, TSM was the dominant factor that correlated more strongly and explained more variations of bulk CPOM and humic-like components than salinity and $\operatorname { C h l } a$

Second, $\Delta a _ { 2 8 0 }$ and the protein-like C3 and C4 also decreased from river to seawater in October and January,which probably indicated inputs of autochthonous POM from freshwater algal production (Brym et al.,2Ol4; Wang et al., 2020a). They were also elevated at some stations of the cultural zone and adjacent seawater in July,which were likely related to the elevated estuarine and marine production as indicated by higher Chl a (Figs.2-4, S4; Table 1 and S1).Previous studies have also shown that the shoulder peak of absorption spectra and the protein-like components were associated with autochthonous production (Brym et al., 2014; Wang et al., 2020a). In addition, C3 showed higher values at some stations in the northern cultural zone in October and January,which mightbe related to the estuarine export,and/or the resuspension of fine sediment during tidal cycles in the clay zone. Although the majority of shellfish have been harvested, the decay and release of soft tissues from the dead bivalves might also in part contribute to the elevated C3 which warrants further studies in the future. Overall, the stepwise multiple linear regression supported that $\Delta a _ { 2 8 0 }$ and C4 were mainly dependent on the Chl a $_ { r : 0 . 9 1 2 }$ and 0.779, respectively),while C3 was affected more by salinity and TSM $( r =$ 0.680,Fig.6).The tyrosine-like C3 and the tryptophan-like C4 have different structures.It has been reported that the tryptophan-like component may indicate less degraded peptide materials than the tyrosine-like component (Fellman et al., 2O1O). The correlation between C4 and Chl $a$ likely indicated freshly produced peptides while C3 might represent more degraded materials from fluvial discharge and/or sediment resuspension in this study.

Finally,the quality indices of POM (S275-295,HIX,and BIX) also showed notable variations (Figs.2-3; Table 1 and S1).HIX decreased from river to seawater in July and October and was highest in October for all three zones,indicating the spatial and seasonal changes of humic content.It correlated positively with a3oo and humic-like components and was mainly dependent on TSM based on the stepwise multiple linear regression $( r = 0 . 8 7 6$ ,Fig.6). S275-295 and BIX both increased from the river to the seawater,but they showed different seasonal variations. $S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 }$ was highest while BIX was lowest in July in both the cultural zone and adjacent seawater.They both increased with increasing salinity and decreasing TSM,which was probably the underlying factor for their similar spatial distribution.In contrast,Chl a correlated positively with $S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 }$ while it correlated negatively with BIX (Table 2). This might result from that algal production led to the increasing $S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 }$ while the degradation of algal organic matter produced the microbial humic-like component and its fluorescence was the numerator in calculating BIX. Thus, the elevated $S _ { 2 7 5 - 2 9 5 }$ and lower BIX in July than in other months for both the cultural zone and adjacent seawater were likelyrelated to the higher Chl $a$ (Fig. S4).

PCA based on the POM parameters and background water quality indices were used to further unveil the underlying mechanisms for the spatial and seasonal changes of POM(Fig.7). Two principal factors (PC1 and PC2) were identified,which explained $6 1 . 6 \%$ and $2 1 . 8 \%$ of the total variance,respectively.PC1 correlated strongly with $a _ { 3 0 0 } ,$ humic-like components (C1,C2,and C5),HIX,and TSM,while it correlated negativelywith salinity and BIX.This suggested thatPC1 mainly represented the bulk POM and humic-like components from terrestrial inputs and sediment resuspension.PC2 correlated positively with the protein-like C4,△a280,S275-295,and Chl $a _ { \mathrm { { i } } }$ ,indicating it was related to the autochthonous production of POM which had abundant protein-like and low molecular weight components.The POM in July was characterized by much higher PC2 values of 0.832-3.380 than in October and January $( - 1 . 3 5 8  – 0 . 0 7 5 )$ ,indicating an overall higher autochthonous production in summer. This was consistent with the seasonal variability of $\operatorname { C h l } a$ which was higher in July $( 6 . 8 1 \pm 2 . 1 1 \mu \mathrm { g } \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 } )$ than in October and January $( 2 . 6 7 \pm 1 . 9 8$ and $1 . 2 2 \pm 0 . 3 7 ~ \mu \mathrm { g } ~ \mathrm { L } ^ { - 1 }$ ,respectively). This was even more evident in most cultural and adjacent seawater stations, where S275-295,△a28o, the protein-like C4,and Chl a were higher in July than in other months (Table 1). It is noteworthy that previous studies have shown the removal of POM by shellfish (Filgueira et al.,2015; Sanz-Lazaro et al., 2O18). The bivalves were seeded in Spring and were cultured until early Autumn (usually in September) in our study area, yet the autochthonous POM was highest in summer.Thus, the elevated aquatic production had a more evident effect on POM than the potential removal by shellfish in July.The POMin October showed the highest PCl in the estuary in all months, which was associated with higher $a _ { 3 0 0 }$ humic-like components (C1,C2,and C5),HIX,and TSM(Figs.2-3).This might be related to either enhanced fluvial discharge or stronger sediment resuspension.However,the fluvial discharge was limited by the low rainfall and discharge in October of the dry season (Fig. S1).In contrast, the sediment in the cultural zone was manually turned over thoroughly during the harvest of shellfish,which might result in stronger sediment resuspension and increase the TSMand associated POM in October.The POM in January was characterized by decreasing PC1 from the estuary to the cultural zone,and to the seawater,which was similar to that in October and indicated decreasing terrestrial influences along the estuary-cultural zone-adjacent seawater continuum. In particular, the low PC2 in October and January indicated no evident effect of POM addition from the laver,which was cultured on the tidal flat after the harvest of bivalves and behaved as a competitor to phytoplankton.The autochthonous POM should be mainly derived from phytoplankton, rather than the epiphyte laver that was fixed on the strings over the tidal flat.The result also indicated that any POM released from the laver culture (e.g.,detritus) was likely degraded,exported to the open sea with lateral advection,and/or settled down to sediments.

# 5．Conclusions

· The quantity and quality of POM showed notable spatial and temporal variations in the estuary-cultural zone-adjacent seawater continuum,which were dependent on different environmental factors. The CPOM and humic-like components were higher in the river end and in October,which correlated most strongly with TSM. $\Delta a _ { 2 8 0 }$ and the protein-like components showed opposite spatial distribution between July and other months,which correlated more strongly with Chl $a _ { * }$   
· The removal of POMby the shellfish in July had a lower effect than the enhanced algal production.The sediment turnover during the shellfish harvest might contribute to the elevated CPOM and humiclike components in October.The laver culture in October and January did not result in a significant addition of autochthonous POM.   
· The mooring monitoring indicated the influences of cross-continuum exchange,sediment resuspension,aquatic production,and respiration on POMin the cultural zone and adjacent seawater. Overall, the biogeochemistry of POM across the estuary-cultural zone-adjacent seawater continuum was closely coupled,and the human disturbance in the cultural zone could affect further the adjacent coastal seawater.
